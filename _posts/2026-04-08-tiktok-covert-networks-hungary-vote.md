TikTok removed covert networks of fake accounts amplifying political content targeting Hungarian users before an upcoming national vote. The operation targeted both opposition leader Péter Magyar and ruling Fidesz, suggesting destabilization rather than support for a single faction.

The multi-directional targeting pattern — running narratives against multiple parties simultaneously — is a noted evolution in influence operations. Platform trust and safety teams and election security researchers should track this approach as it reduces attribution signals typically associated with state-sponsored interference.
