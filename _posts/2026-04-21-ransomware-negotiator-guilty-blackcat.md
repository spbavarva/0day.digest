A professional ransomware negotiator has pleaded guilty to charges related to participation in the
BlackCat (ALPHV) ransomware scheme, in a case that illustrates the risk of entanglement between
negotiation and ransom payment channels. Experts noted that negotiators must maintain strict
independence from payment processing to avoid conflicts of interest that can cross into criminality.

For organizations that engage third-party ransomware negotiators during incidents, this case is a
useful reminder to vet providers carefully, establish separation of duties between negotiation and
financial transactions, and include accountability requirements in incident response vendor
contracts.
