A breach of the Los Angeles city attorney's office systems exposed sensitive LAPD files stored in the city's document management infrastructure. Social media posts — some since removed — advertised 7.7 terabytes of data available for download.

The scale and nature of the dataset raises significant concerns for active investigations, officer identities, and witness information. Law enforcement agencies sharing systems with other government offices should review access controls and data segmentation policies. The posting of download links on social media indicates active exfiltration and distribution.
