Iran-linked threat actors have publicly vowed to resume cyberattacks against American targets when geopolitical conditions allow, framing digital operations as an integral component of military strategy rather than a separate domain. SecurityWeek assesses that ceasefire periods are likely used for regrouping rather than standing down.

Historical patterns show Iranian APT groups use geopolitical pauses to retool and pre-position for future campaigns. Critical infrastructure operators and government agencies should maintain elevated threat posture and treat the current period as a preparation window rather than a reduced-threat environment.
