A vulnerability in FIFA's streaming infrastructure could have let an
attacker remotely take over World Cup broadcasts, according to Dark
Reading. The root cause was unenforced Microsoft Entra access controls,
which a hacker could have abused to hijack or deface live streams. The
case illustrates how identity and access-control misconfigurations on
high-profile public infrastructure carry outsized risk during major live
events.
