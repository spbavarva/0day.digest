Researchers have linked the four-year-old "Popa" botnet — which has forced
millions of Android-based consumer TV boxes to relay internet traffic — to
NetNut, a residential proxy provider operated by publicly-traded Israeli
firm Alarum Technologies (NASDAQ: ALAR). Traffic relayed through the
compromised devices has reportedly supported advertising fraud, account
takeovers, and mass data-scraping operations. Multiple security firms
contributed to the investigation tying the botnet to NetNut.
