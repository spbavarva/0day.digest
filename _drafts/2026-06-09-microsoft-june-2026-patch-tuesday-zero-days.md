Microsoft's June 2026 Patch Tuesday addresses 200 security vulnerabilities across Windows and related products.
Three of the patched flaws were publicly disclosed before fixes were released, qualifying them as zero-days; exploitation status of these three was not confirmed at time of disclosure.
Windows 10 (KB5094127) and Windows 11 (KB5094126 / KB5093998) cumulative updates have been released; the Windows 10 update also begins rolling out updated Secure Boot certificates to replace expiring ones.
Prioritize the three publicly disclosed CVEs in your patch schedule; Secure Boot certificate changes may require testing before broad deployment in managed environments.
