Researchers found 28 fraudulent Android apps on the official Google Play Store that falsely
promised access to any phone number's call history, then enrolled users in fake subscriptions that
delivered fabricated data. The apps collectively reached 7.3 million downloads, with a single app
exceeding 1 million installs. Google has been notified. Android users should review active
subscriptions in Play Store billing settings and uninstall any call-history lookup or "phone
tracker" type applications.
