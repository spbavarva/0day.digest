The UK AI Safety Institute's independent evaluation of Claude Mythos Preview confirms the model
is "exceptionally effective" at identifying security vulnerabilities, backing up Anthropic's own
capability claims. The AISI evaluation also found that model performance scales with token spend:
the more compute thrown at a security review, the better the results.

Analyst Drew Breunig observes this creates a pay-to-win dynamic in offensive security — attackers
and defenders alike will use AI spending as a competitive lever, with well-funded actors gaining
disproportionate advantage. The implication: AI-assisted vulnerability discovery will be
asymmetrically available to those who can afford to run it at scale.

Security teams should factor AI-assisted discovery into their threat models and begin evaluating
whether their own AI review budgets can pace with attacker economics. The CSA is calling this the
"Mythos-Ready" challenge (see companion post).
