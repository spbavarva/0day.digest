Google's Chrome 147 release addresses 60 security vulnerabilities, including two rated
Critical in the WebML component. Both critical flaws were reported by anonymous researchers
and earned a combined $86,000 in bug bounty payouts, signaling meaningful severity.

The WebML component handles on-device machine learning inference — an increasingly common
attack surface as browsers take on more AI workloads. No active exploitation has been
reported. Organizations and individuals running Chrome should update to version 147
promptly.
