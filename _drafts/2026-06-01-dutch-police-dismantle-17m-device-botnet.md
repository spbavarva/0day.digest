Dutch authorities seized command-and-control servers tied to a botnet of 17 million infected computers, smartphones, and tablets, allegedly operated as a residential proxy network to facilitate cybercrime. Residential proxy networks route malicious traffic through infected consumer devices, allowing operators to present legitimate residential IP addresses and defeat IP-based detection and blocking.

The takedown is one of the largest residential proxy botnet seizures recorded. Organizations conducting fraud detection, abuse monitoring, or bot mitigation should note that proxy-based traffic obfuscation through infected consumer devices remains a primary evasion technique.
