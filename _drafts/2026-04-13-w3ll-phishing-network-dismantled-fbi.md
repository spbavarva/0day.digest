The FBI and Indonesian National Police have jointly dismantled the W3LL phishing network in
the first coordinated US-Indonesia enforcement action targeting a phishing kit developer. W3LL
offered an off-the-shelf phishing toolkit for approximately $500 enabling creation of convincing
fake login portals, and is linked to over $20 million in fraud attempts affecting thousands of
victims. The alleged developer has been detained.

W3LL's toolkit was notable for its accessibility — lowering the barrier for credential theft
campaigns to non-technical actors. The takedown removes active infrastructure, but the
underlying kit has been widely distributed. Security teams should continue monitoring for
W3LL-pattern phishing pages targeting their users, particularly credential-harvesting portals
mimicking enterprise SSO and email providers.
