Dark Reading reports attackers built a cross-platform clipboard hijacker campaign by manufacturing fake trust signals across GitHub, YouTube, and VirusTotal to make malicious tools look legitimate. The fabricated reputation-boosting effort helped the malware evade scrutiny long enough to facilitate a cryptocurrency heist. The case is a reminder that platform trust signals like stars, views, and scan results can be gamed and shouldn't be the sole basis for trusting a tool.
