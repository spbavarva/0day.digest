The FCC has extended its planned ban on security updates for foreign-manufactured routers
and drones from the original March 2027 deadline to at least January 1, 2029. The ban
targets hardware from vendors deemed national security risks — primarily Chinese manufacturers
— and would prohibit firmware updates that could introduce backdoors or malicious code.

The delay was announced by the FCC's Office of Engineering and Technology. Network operators
running regulated environments or government-adjacent infrastructure should factor the
revised 2029 deadline into hardware replacement planning, while noting that the regulatory
direction remains unchanged and delayed compliance deadlines do not reduce the underlying
security risk of keeping affected hardware in service.
