A new ransomware operation tracked as "Prinz Eugen" has been observed
prioritizing recently modified files for encryption, on the assumption that
newer files are more valuable to victims and increase pressure to pay.
Unlike most ransomware families, it does not drop a ransom note on the
infected system, leaving victims without an immediate explanation of the
attack. Details on the operation's distribution method, initial access
vector, and ransom demands are not yet public. Defenders should watch for
unusual rapid encryption activity concentrated on recently touched files.
