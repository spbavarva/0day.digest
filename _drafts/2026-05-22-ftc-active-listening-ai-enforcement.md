The FTC has ordered Cox Media Group and two other firms to pay nearly $1 million to settle charges
that they deceived customers about an AI-powered "active listening" marketing service. The product
allegedly claimed smart devices capture real-time voice data which is then paired with behavioral
data to target advertising — a capability Cox was marketing to advertisers without disclosing this
to consumers.

The FTC action confirms that regulators will pursue enforcement when AI marketing claims imply
undisclosed surveillance capabilities. It is a notable data point for any company building or
selling AI products that touch user audio or behavioral data.
