Tyler Robert Buchanan, 24, a British national who operated under the alias "Tylerb," has pleaded
guilty to wire fraud conspiracy and aggravated identity theft. Buchanan was a senior member of
the Scattered Spider cybercrime group, responsible for SMS-based phishing campaigns in summer
2022 that compromised at least a dozen major technology companies and led to the theft of tens of
millions of dollars in cryptocurrency.

Scattered Spider's attacks are notable for their reliance on social engineering rather than
technical exploits: the group targeted IT helpdesks, identity providers, and support staff using
convincing impersonation and SIM swapping to take over employee accounts. The breach of multiple
large tech companies via these methods highlights that strong perimeter defenses provide limited
protection when the identity layer is manipulated.

The plea is a significant legal outcome for a group that operated with apparent impunity for
years. Organizations should revisit helpdesk verification procedures, SIM swap protections with
mobile carriers, and phishing-resistant MFA implementation in response to the documented
Scattered Spider playbook.
