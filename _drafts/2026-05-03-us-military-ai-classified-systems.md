The US Department of Defense announced agreements with seven commercial tech companies — Google, Microsoft, AWS, Nvidia, OpenAI, Reflection, and SpaceX — to deploy AI on classified government systems. The stated goal is augmenting warfighter decision-making in complex operational environments.

This is the most concrete signal yet that frontier commercial AI is moving into sensitive national security infrastructure at scale. The deals imply each vendor must meet DoD classified systems authorization requirements, which carry significant implications for data handling, model auditability, and access controls. Security architects working on federal or defense-adjacent cloud environments should expect increased scrutiny around AI workloads in classified enclaves.
