Jacob Butler, 23, of Ottawa, Canada — known online as "Dort" — was arrested by Canadian authorities
on suspicion of building and operating the Kimwolf botnet, a variant of AISURU that infected nearly
two million devices globally. The botnet was used for DDoS-for-hire attacks over the past six
months. He faces criminal hacking charges in both Canada and the United States.

Krebs on Security had publicly named Butler as a suspect in February 2026 after Kimwolf was used to
launch DDoS, doxing, and swatting attacks against Krebs and other researchers. The arrest is a rare
case where a botnet operator was publicly identified prior to formal charges.
