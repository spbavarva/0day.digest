A previously undocumented data-wiping malware called Lotus was deployed in targeted attacks against
energy and utility organizations in Venezuela during 2025. Unlike ransomware, wipers are designed
to destroy data rather than encrypt it for ransom — a destructive pattern associated with
nation-state actors seeking operational disruption rather than financial gain.

The targeting of energy and utility critical infrastructure with a novel, purpose-built wiper is a
significant development for ICS and OT security teams. Organizations in energy, utilities, and
critical infrastructure should review indicators of compromise from this report, assess backup and
recovery posture against destructive attack scenarios, and evaluate whether existing detection
coverage would identify wiper-class activity on OT-adjacent systems.
