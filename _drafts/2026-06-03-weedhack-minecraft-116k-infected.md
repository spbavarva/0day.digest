The WeedHack malware-as-a-service campaign has infected more than 116,000 Windows systems since January 2026 by distributing trojanized Minecraft clients and mods through YouTube video tutorials. The MaaS operation grants operators full system control over victim machines and has been active for over five months. Distribution via gaming tutorial content reaches a demographic that frequently runs with local admin privileges and has limited security tooling. Users should obtain Minecraft clients exclusively from official channels (minecraft.net) and treat mod download links from YouTube descriptions as high-risk. Endpoint protection with behavioral detection is the most effective mitigation given the campaign's commodity tooling.
