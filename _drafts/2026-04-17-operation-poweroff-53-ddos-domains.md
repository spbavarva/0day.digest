An international law enforcement operation called Operation PowerOFF, involving 21
countries, has seized 53 domains hosting DDoS-for-hire services, arrested four individuals,
and obtained access to account data for approximately 3 million users of the criminal
booter infrastructure.

The services had been used by more than 75,000 cybercriminals. The disruption targets
the commercial layer of the DDoS ecosystem — services that allow non-technical actors to
launch disruptive attacks without operating their own botnet infrastructure.

The exposure of 3 million user accounts may lead to follow-on law enforcement actions
against customers of the seized services.
