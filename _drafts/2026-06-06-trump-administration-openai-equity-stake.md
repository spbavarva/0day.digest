President Trump stated he is in discussions about deals "where the American people
can benefit from the success of AI," raising the possibility of a U.S. government
equity stake in OpenAI. No formal agreement has been announced.

A government equity position in a frontier AI lab would be a novel governance
arrangement with significant implications for AI regulation, antitrust enforcement,
and international AI competition. The development coincides with the departure of
Sriram Krishnan from his White House AI advisor role; Krishnan reportedly plans to
start a new institution focused on shaping U.S. AI policy.
