The FTC will ban data broker Kochava and its subsidiary Collective Data Solutions from selling precise geolocation data collected from hundreds of millions of mobile devices without consumers' explicit consent. The action settles charges that Kochava sold location data granular enough to track individuals to sensitive locations including homes, places of worship, and healthcare providers. This is one of the FTC's most significant data broker enforcement actions and follows a multi-year investigation. Mobile app developers using Kochava's attribution SDK should review their data sharing agreements and privacy disclosures in light of this enforcement outcome.
