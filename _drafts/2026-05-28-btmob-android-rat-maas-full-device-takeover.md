A new Android RAT named BTMOB combines financial theft, data exfiltration, and
remote access to achieve full device takeover. It is distributed via phishing
lures under a Malware-as-a-Service (MaaS) model that provides operators with a
no-code malware development interface, lowering the barrier to entry for less
technical attackers. The RAT has been observed targeting users primarily in Brazil
and Latin America. Android users in the region should be cautious of unsolicited
download prompts; enterprise security teams should ensure mobile device management
policies block sideloading of unknown APKs.
