Krebs on Security reports that a Brazilian DDoS protection firm was itself compromised, with the breach enabling a sustained botnet campaign of massive DDoS attacks against competing ISPs in Brazil. The firm's CEO attributes the activity to a competitor using the breach as a sabotage vector, though the full scope of the intrusion has not been disclosed.

The incident illustrates a recurring pattern: security vendors' network visibility, infrastructure access, and trusted position in customer environments make them high-value targets. Organizations relying on third-party DDoS mitigation providers should periodically assess the security posture of those vendors, and ensure that mitigation contracts include clear incident notification requirements.
