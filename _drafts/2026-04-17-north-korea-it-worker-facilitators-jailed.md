Two individuals — Kejia Wang and Zhenxing Wang — have been sentenced to prison in the US
for their roles in a North Korean IT worker infiltration scheme. They compromised the
identities of dozens of American citizens to help North Korean workers land remote
employment at over 100 US companies.

The scheme is part of a broader DPRK effort to embed IT workers inside Western technology
firms, generating revenue for the regime and potentially enabling insider access to
sensitive systems, source code, and intellectual property.

Organizations hiring remote software engineers should apply enhanced identity verification
during onboarding, particularly for fully remote positions. This case follows multiple
prior prosecutions in the same scheme and signals continued operational activity.
