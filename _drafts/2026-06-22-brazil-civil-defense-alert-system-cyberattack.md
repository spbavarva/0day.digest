At least a dozen unauthorized alerts were pushed through Brazil's Civil Defense Alert system in a suspected cyberattack early Saturday. The platform is designed to warn residents about floods, landslides, and other natural disasters. The intrusion vector has not yet been disclosed, and the incident remains under investigation. Unauthorized access to public emergency-alert infrastructure raises concerns about both public panic and erosion of trust in legitimate warnings.
