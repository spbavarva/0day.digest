Researchers found more than 1 million phishing emails using hidden text —
sometimes called text salting — to evade AI and LLM-based email security
filters. The hidden content is invisible to the recipient but changes how
the message is parsed by AI classifiers, letting phishing payloads slide
past detection and into inboxes. The finding highlights a gap in AI-based
security tooling that traditional signature and heuristic filters may not
share.
