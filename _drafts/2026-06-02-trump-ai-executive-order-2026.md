President Trump signed an executive order creating a voluntary framework under which AI companies may share frontier models with the federal government before public release. The order emphasizes cybersecurity protections for shared models and positions prerelease access as a means to strengthen critical infrastructure defense. The order is significantly narrower than earlier drafts following industry objections — participation is voluntary, with no mandatory review timelines or safety thresholds specified. Federal access to models is subject to confidentiality, cybersecurity, and nondisclosure requirements. The order reflects ongoing tension between federal oversight ambitions and industry pressure against mandatory gatekeeping.
