Dashlane disclosed that on May 31, 2026 an external threat actor launched a brute-force
attack against personal subscription accounts, targeting two-factor authentication to
download encrypted vaults. Fewer than 20 users had vaults successfully downloaded.

Dashlane's systems automatically locked affected accounts. Downloaded vaults remain
encrypted and are not accessible without the user's master password, which Dashlane
does not store. Affected users have been notified. The incident scope is narrow but
notable given the sensitivity of password manager data.
