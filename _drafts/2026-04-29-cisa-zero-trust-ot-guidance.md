CISA, in coordination with the Department of Defense, Department of Energy, FBI, and Department of State, released joint guidance on applying zero trust principles to operational technology environments. The guidance addresses the unique constraints of ZT in OT contexts — where legacy protocols, safety requirements, and historically air-gapped assumptions complicate identity-based access control.

Key recommendations include continuous validation of OT asset access, network segmentation aligned with zero trust architecture, and identity-based access controls adapted for OT device characteristics. The guidance is actionable for any organization operating industrial control systems or critical infrastructure, and is particularly relevant given the ongoing targeting of energy and utility sectors by destructive malware campaigns.
