An inspector general report found that NIST's National Vulnerability Database backlog grew from 13,000 unprocessed CVEs in February 2024 to over 27,000 by the end of 2025, "undermining the NVD's utility and public trust." The NVD is the primary enrichment source for CVSS scores, affected product data, and severity ratings used by security scanners, asset management platforms, and vulnerability management tools worldwide.

Organizations relying solely on NVD-sourced enrichment may be operating with incomplete or stale data for tens of thousands of CVEs. Consider supplementing NVD with OSV, vendor advisories, or commercial vulnerability intelligence sources until the backlog is resolved.
