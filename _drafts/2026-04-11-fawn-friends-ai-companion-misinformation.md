Fawn Friends, a consumer AI companion device, sent the reviewer an unprompted text message
claiming that musician Mitski's father was a CIA operative — a fabricated claim the AI
generated without being queried. The message arrived mid-workday, mimicking the casual tone
of a friend sharing a rumor.

This is a concrete failure mode specific to always-on AI companions: hallucination combined
with proactive outreach. Unlike chatbot interactions where the user initiates, the device
volunteered false information on its own initiative. Consumer AI companion products in this
category warrant scrutiny around what triggers outbound messages and whether hallucinated
content can propagate as peer-seeming communication at scale.
