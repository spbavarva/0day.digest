The FBI has warned US transportation and logistics companies of a sharp rise in cyber-enabled cargo theft, with combined US and Canadian losses reaching nearly $725 million in 2025. Criminal groups use fraudulent digital identities, fake carrier profiles, and business email compromise tactics to intercept and divert shipments in transit by impersonating legitimate logistics participants.

The attack model targets the weak identity verification in freight brokerage workflows. Organizations in logistics should enforce secondary verification for last-minute carrier substitutions, validate broker credentials through out-of-band channels, and treat anomalous load tender modifications as a red flag for interception attempts.
