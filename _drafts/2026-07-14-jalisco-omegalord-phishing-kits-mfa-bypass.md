Two new phishing kits, dubbed Jalisco and OmegaLord, have been observed in campaigns targeting Microsoft 365 accounts using techniques designed to defeat multi-factor authentication. The kits are part of a broader trend of adversary-in-the-middle phishing infrastructure built to intercept and replay MFA-protected sessions. The specific bypass mechanism was not fully detailed in initial reporting. Organizations should consider phishing-resistant authentication (FIDO2/passkeys) for high-value M365 accounts, since traditional MFA is increasingly targeted by kits like these.
