OpenAI's former CTO Mira Murati testified under oath during the Musk v. Altman trial that CEO Sam Altman falsely told her that OpenAI's legal department had approved a new AI model from a safety standards perspective. Murati said she could not trust Altman's words based on this and other incidents.

The testimony is significant for AI governance: it suggests that executive communications about internal safety reviews at a leading AI lab may not be reliable. For practitioners relying on AI provider safety commitments, this strengthens the case for independent third-party safety audits rather than self-reported compliance.
