Sweden has for the first time publicly attributed a cyberattack on its energy infrastructure to
a pro-Russian threat group. The attack targeted a district heating plant in western Sweden and
occurred in 2025; the announcement marks Sweden's first official public attribution of a
state-linked cyber incident against energy infrastructure.

Public attribution by a NATO member of an OT attack against civilian energy infrastructure is
geopolitically significant. District heating plants are interconnected systems serving large
numbers of residents, and disruption during cold months can cascade into public health impacts.
The disclosure comes amid a broader pattern of Russian-aligned threat actors targeting European
energy and utilities.

Energy sector operators should review threat intelligence on pro-Russian OT-capable threat
actors, implement IT/OT network segmentation, and ensure ICS incident response playbooks are
current. ENISA's OT threat landscape and CISA's ICS advisories provide relevant frameworks.
