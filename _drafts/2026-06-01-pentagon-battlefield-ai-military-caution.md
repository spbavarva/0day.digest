The Pentagon is accelerating deployment of AI systems in battlefield and military decision-making contexts, framing AI capability as a unique American strategic advantage. Some senior military leaders are publicly urging caution, raising concerns about autonomous systems operating in lethal environments without sufficient human oversight. The debate parallels civilian AI safety discussions but with uniquely irreversible stakes — errors in autonomous weapons systems cannot be rolled back. The tension between speed-of-deployment pressure and safety oversight mirrors dynamics seen in commercial AI, now playing out with higher consequences.
