WatchGuard and ESET have documented concurrent banking trojan campaigns:
Grandoreiro targeting Windows users in Spain, Portugal, and Mexico, and BTMOB
RAT targeting Android users in Brazil.

Grandoreiro is a persistent Latin American banking trojan that resurfaces with
updated distribution and evasion techniques; BTMOB RAT is an Android remote
access trojan that intercepts banking credentials and OTP codes from mobile
banking apps. Financial institutions and their customers in the targeted regions
should apply heightened fraud monitoring during the active campaign window.
