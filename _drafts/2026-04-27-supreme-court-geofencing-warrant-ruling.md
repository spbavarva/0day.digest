During oral arguments, U.S. Supreme Court justices signaled strong support for requiring law enforcement to obtain a warrant before conducting geofence searches — reverse location data requests to providers like Google that can expose the movements of thousands of individuals in a given area and time window. Privacy advocates had warned that a ruling in the other direction would open the door to broad warrantless surveillance via geofencing.

A formal ruling is expected before the current term ends. If the court extends Fourth Amendment protections to geofence warrants, law enforcement agencies that rely heavily on reverse-search requests will need to adjust investigative workflows. Organizations that respond to such requests as data custodians should monitor the outcome, as it will affect compliance obligations around law enforcement data requests.
