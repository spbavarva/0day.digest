New research reported by Dark Reading found that identity-based attacks —
such as phishing and credential theft — overtook exploited vulnerabilities
as the top root cause of ransomware incidents last year. Multi-factor
authentication was deployed in 97% of the credential-based attacks studied
but still failed to prevent compromise in those cases. The finding suggests
MFA alone is no longer sufficient protection against identity-focused
ransomware entry. Organizations should treat MFA as necessary but
insufficient, and invest in phishing-resistant authentication and identity
threat detection alongside patch management.
