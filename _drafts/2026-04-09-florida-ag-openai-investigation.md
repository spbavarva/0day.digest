Florida Attorney General James Uthmeier has launched a formal investigation into OpenAI,
citing concerns that OpenAI's data and technology may be accessible to adversarial foreign
governments, specifically naming the Chinese Communist Party. The investigation was reportedly
prompted in part by a 2025 mass shooting at Florida State University in which the suspect
allegedly used ChatGPT to plan the attack; one victim's family has announced plans to sue OpenAI.

The action represents a broadening of state-level regulatory pressure on AI companies beyond
the federal level, and introduces product liability framing alongside the national security angle.
The outcome could set precedent for how AI providers are held accountable for downstream harms
involving model outputs and for data governance practices with foreign adversary implications.
