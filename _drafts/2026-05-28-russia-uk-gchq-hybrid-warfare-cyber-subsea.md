Anne Keast-Butler, director of GCHQ, publicly disclosed that Russia is conducting
daily attacks on the UK spanning subsea cable interference, energy pipeline sabotage,
and cyber operations. GCHQ is actively defending subsea cable infrastructure,
disrupting Russian networks smuggling sanctioned technology, and countering
assassination attempts on UK soil. The unusually candid public statement is a signal
that the UK government views the threat as warranting public awareness. Security
teams in UK critical infrastructure sectors should review their threat models for
Russian hybrid tactics including physical sabotage combined with cyber disruption.
