The White House issued National Security Presidential Memorandum 12 (NSPM-12), establishing a governance and accountability structure for National Security System (NSS) cybersecurity and reestablishing the Committee on National Security Systems (CNSS). NSS covers classified and sensitive government networks used for defense, intelligence, and foreign policy — systems that sit outside the civilian FISMA/CISA authority framework. NSPM-12 clarifies authority lines for protecting these networks and sets accountability expectations for agencies operating them. The memo's practical impact depends on implementation details and enforcement mechanisms not yet made public. It signals renewed executive attention to the security of the most sensitive federal infrastructure.
