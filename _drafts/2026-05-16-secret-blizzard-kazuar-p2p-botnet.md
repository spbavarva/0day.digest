Russian threat actor Secret Blizzard has significantly upgraded its Kazuar backdoor into a modular peer-to-peer botnet architecture designed for long-term persistence, stealth, and sustained data collection.

The P2P design eliminates dependence on centralized C2 infrastructure, making takedowns more difficult and network-level detection harder. This follows a broader pattern of Russian APTs investing in durable, resilient implants built for longevity over speed. Defenders should review threat intel feeds for updated Kazuar IOCs and monitor for anomalous peer-to-peer traffic patterns on sensitive hosts.
