France Titres, the French government agency responsible for issuing administrative documents
including identity cards, passports, and driver's licenses, has confirmed a data breach after a
threat actor claimed the attack and began offering stolen citizen data for sale. The agency manages
identity documentation for the entire French population.

A breach of a national document issuance authority exposes data that cannot easily be rotated or
changed — leaked identity document data enables persistent identity fraud and targeted phishing.
Organizations that process or verify French citizen credentials should heighten document
verification scrutiny. Details on the breach scope and affected data types are still emerging.
