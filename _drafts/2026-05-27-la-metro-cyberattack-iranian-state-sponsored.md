A cyberattack against LA Metro was publicly claimed by a hacktivist group,
but investigators have linked the attack infrastructure to Iranian government
threat actors. The hacktivist group served as a front to obscure the
state-sponsored origin of the operation.

Using hacktivist personas to mask state-sponsored activity is a documented
Iranian tradecraft pattern, allowing deniability while maintaining operational
impact against critical infrastructure targets.

Security teams at critical infrastructure organizations should treat
hacktivist attribution claims with skepticism when technical indicators
suggest a more sophisticated underlying actor.
