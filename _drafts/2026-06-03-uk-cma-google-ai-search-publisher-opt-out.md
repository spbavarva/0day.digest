The UK Competition and Markets Authority issued a new conduct rule requiring Google to allow website publishers to exclude their content from AI Search features — including AI Overviews — without losing placement in standard search results. Previously, publishers faced an all-or-nothing choice between appearing in AI summaries and appearing in search. The rule is a significant win for publishers contesting AI-powered scraping of their content without compensation. The CMA's action is likely to be cited as precedent in parallel proceedings in the EU and United States. Google must implement opt-out mechanisms within the timeline specified by the CMA.
