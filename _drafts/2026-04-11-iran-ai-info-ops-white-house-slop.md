During the early phase of the Iran conflict, U.S. government social media accounts posted
Call of Duty memes and AI-generated dancing bowling pins. Iranian state media simultaneously
flooded platforms with authentic footage: explosions over Tehran, smoke, and on-the-ground
video. By most accounts, Iran's information operation landed harder.

The contrast illustrates a strategic risk in overreliance on AI-generated content for
wartime communications: AI slop is visually identifiable, tonally dissonant in high-stakes
contexts, and erodes credibility precisely when credibility matters most. Defenders and
communicators building AI-assisted content pipelines should weigh authenticity signals
against production speed, particularly in crisis scenarios where adversaries can cheaply
undercut with raw documentation.
