SecurityWeek's weekly roundup covers several notable incidents: a Windows zero-day
vulnerability is under active investigation; medical device maker Stryker confirmed a
cyberattack; law firm Jones Day was breached; and a supercomputer in China was
reportedly compromised. On the tooling side, the Internet Bug Bounty program has paused
new submissions citing concerns about AI-generated reports overwhelming the queue. A
new Mac stealer malware family is also being actively analyzed. Further technical
details on the Windows zero-day are expected as Microsoft's investigation continues;
defenders should monitor Microsoft's security advisories for an out-of-band patch.
