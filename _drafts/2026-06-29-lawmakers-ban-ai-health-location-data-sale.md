Sen. Elizabeth Warren (D-MA) and Rep. Mary Gay Scanlon (D-PA) plan to
introduce an updated Health and Location Data Protection Act in the coming
weeks. The bill would bar the sale of Americans' health and location data to
data brokers, including information people share with AI chatbots such as
ChatGPT or Claude.

The proposal targets a gap where conversational disclosures to AI assistants
could otherwise be treated as sellable data broker inventory.
