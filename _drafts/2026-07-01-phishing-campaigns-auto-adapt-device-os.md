Attackers are increasingly fingerprinting victims through user-agent
data to tailor phishing payloads to their specific device and operating
system. By detecting the victim's platform before serving a malicious
link or file, attackers can deliver OS-specific malware or a fake login
page matched to what the target expects to see. Researchers say this
device-aware targeting is increasing both compromise rates and overall
campaign profitability compared to generic, one-size-fits-all lures.
Defenders should treat user-agent based content-switching on phishing
infrastructure as a sign of a more sophisticated operation.
