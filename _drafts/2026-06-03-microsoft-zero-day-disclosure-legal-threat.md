Microsoft issued a public response after reports emerged that it had threatened legal action against security researchers who publicly disclose zero-day vulnerabilities in Microsoft products before patches are available. The backlash from the vulnerability research community cited chilling effects on coordinated disclosure and responsible reporting practices. Microsoft's statement attempts to reassure researchers that responsible disclosure channels remain open, but did not announce a concrete policy change or clarify the specific circumstances that prompted the legal threats. The incident reignites long-standing tension between large software vendors and independent vulnerability researchers over disclosure timelines and liability.
