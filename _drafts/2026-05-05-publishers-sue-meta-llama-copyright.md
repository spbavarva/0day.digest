Five major publishers — Macmillan, McGraw Hill, Elsevier, Hachette, and one additional plaintiff — along with one author have filed a class action against Meta alleging the company engaged in one of the largest copyright infringements in history when training its Llama AI models. The suit, first reported by The New York Times, claims Meta reproduced copyrighted books "word-for-word" without licensing. This case joins a growing body of copyright litigation against AI companies including The New York Times v. OpenAI. A ruling or settlement could set precedent affecting how all major AI labs handle training data from commercial publishers.
