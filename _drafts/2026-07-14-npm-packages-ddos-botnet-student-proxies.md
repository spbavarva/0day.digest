JFrog researchers found a campaign of 148 npm packages disguised as free
web proxies marketed to students, used as free registry-hosted static
content rather than installable dependencies. Visitors to the linked proxy
site had their browsers silently conscripted into a distributed
denial-of-service botnet. The campaign ran for roughly two weeks in May
2026. Developers who install packages were not the target — students
seeking to bypass network restrictions were the ones affected.
