AI agents capable of autonomously discovering and exploiting obscure vulnerabilities are emerging alongside a flood of potentially flawed AI-generated code, creating a compounding challenge for defenders. The expansion of AI in both offense and development is widening attack surfaces at a rate that outpaces traditional vulnerability management workflows.

Dark Reading argues that defenders must adapt tooling and operational practices to contend with AI-speed adversaries. Security teams evaluating their detection and response capabilities should factor in AI-generated code review and autonomous exploitation as near-term threats rather than theoretical ones.
