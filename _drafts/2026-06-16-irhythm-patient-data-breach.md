Digital healthcare company iRhythm Holdings disclosed a data breach in which hackers stole patients' personal and health information stored on third-party-hosted business applications. iRhythm manufactures the Zio patch, a wearable cardiac monitoring device used by millions of patients across the US. The breach impacts patient data including personal identifiers and health information; iRhythm has not yet disclosed the number of affected individuals or the specific data categories involved. Third-party hosted business applications remain a persistent attack vector in healthcare. Affected patients and providers should monitor for communications from iRhythm and remain alert to targeted phishing using leaked health data.
