Researchers ran 281 popular free VPN apps from the Google Play Store
through a new testing system and found many fail at basic privacy
protections, the core function users install a VPN for. At least 29 of
the flagged apps let user traffic leak outside the encrypted tunnel. The
apps identified with at least one problem have a combined install base of
more than 2.4 billion. Users relying on free Android VPN apps for privacy
should verify the app's traffic-leak behavior rather than assume the VPN
label guarantees protection.
