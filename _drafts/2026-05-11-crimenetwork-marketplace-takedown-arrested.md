German law enforcement shut down the second iteration of Crimenetwork, a German-language online crime
marketplace that had rebuilt after a prior takedown and grown to over 22,000 registered users with more than
100 active sellers. The platform's administrator was arrested as part of the coordinated operation.

Crimenetwork was a significant venue for cybercrime-as-a-service in German-speaking Europe, reportedly offering
credentials, fraud tooling, and illicit services. Takedowns of this scale displace users to competing forums
rather than eliminating demand, but represent meaningful disruption and signal continued law enforcement focus
on German-language criminal infrastructure.
