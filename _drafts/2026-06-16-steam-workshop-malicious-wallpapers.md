Kaspersky GReAT researchers found dozens of malicious wallpapers uploaded to Steam Workshop since late 2025 that deploy malware on download, primarily targeting gamers in China and Russia. The attackers abuse Steam's trusted content-sharing platform — users expect custom wallpapers and cosmetics, not executables hidden within them. Steam Workshop does not apply the same vetting used for the main Steam store, making it an effective malware distribution channel with a built-in audience. Valve has not publicly responded to the findings. Gamers should be cautious about installing Workshop content from unknown or low-reputation authors, particularly cosmetic items with no functional reason to execute code.
