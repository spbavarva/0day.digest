Cyberattack attempts targeting critical infrastructure in the United Arab Emirates tripled within a matter of weeks as the broader Middle East conflict extends into the digital domain. The surge tracks with ongoing regional tensions and suggests state-affiliated or state-directed actors are broadening their operational scope beyond direct conflict participants. Critical infrastructure sectors — including energy, water, and transportation — appear to be primary targets. Organizations operating or supporting infrastructure in the Gulf region should review their threat posture, ensure OT/IT segmentation is intact, and heighten monitoring for anomalous lateral movement or access attempts.
