The Bluekit phishing-as-a-service platform has added browser-in-the-middle
capability, letting operators relay a victim's live session and steal
credentials (and session cookies) in real time rather than just harvesting
typed passwords. Nearly 70 new hostnames tied to the kit were identified in
the past week alone, reflecting rapid infrastructure turnover. The
technique helps Bluekit-based phishing pages defeat simple password-only
defenses, making MFA-aware monitoring and BitM proxy detection more
important for defenders tracking this kit.
