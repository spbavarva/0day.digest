A North Korean threat group stole more than $12 million in cryptocurrency from individual users
during the first three months of 2026 through malware attacks targeting personal devices. The
campaign reflects the continued reliance of DPRK-affiliated actors on crypto theft as a primary
revenue stream, a pattern that has persisted across multiple years and named threat groups.

Targets holding cryptocurrency on personal devices — particularly non-custodial wallets — remain
the primary attack surface. Standard defenses apply: hardware wallets for significant holdings,
scrutiny of any unsolicited executables or browser extensions, and keeping device software current.
