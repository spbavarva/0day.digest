Researchers report that GreyVibe, a Russia-linked threat group, extensively
integrates ChatGPT, Gemini, and other AI tools into its cyberattack operations.
The group's use of commercial AI platforms for reconnaissance, content generation,
and tooling development offers an early look at how state-aligned actors will
operationalize AI at scale. Defenders should expect AI to accelerate attack
velocity, lower the cost of social engineering, and enable more convincing phishing
lures. Security teams should review AI usage policies for potential abuse of
corporate AI subscriptions and monitor for AI-generated content patterns in spear
phishing campaigns targeting their organizations.
