Infoblox researchers have detailed a telecommunications fraud operation using fake CAPTCHA
verification pages to trick users into triggering International Revenue Share Fraud (IRSF) — 
victims unknowingly send premium-rate international SMS messages that generate revenue for threat
actors leasing the phone numbers. The same infrastructure underpins at least 120 distinct Keitaro
traffic distribution campaigns also linked to cryptocurrency fraud.

The fake CAPTCHA lure is a departure from credential-phishing; the goal is covert premium SMS
activation rather than account takeover. The scale — 120 Keitaro campaigns — suggests a
commoditized fraud-as-a-service operation. Mobile users on networks without SMS fraud controls and
organizations with BYOD policies are the primary exposure surface.
