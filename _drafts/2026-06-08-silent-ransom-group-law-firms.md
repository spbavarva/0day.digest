The financially motivated Silent Ransom Group is targeting US law firms with a
multi-vector extortion campaign combining vishing calls, IT impersonation over
phone and email, and physical in-person office intrusions where operatives pose
as IT staff. The physical component is an unusual escalation beyond typical
cyber-extortion tactics and suggests a well-resourced, operationally
sophisticated group. Law firm security teams should brief reception and
facilities staff on verifying IT personnel credentials before granting access,
review visitor badge and access-control logs, and ensure sensitive data access
requires out-of-band MFA that cannot be socially engineered via phone or
in-person requests.
