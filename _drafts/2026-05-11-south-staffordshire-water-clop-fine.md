The UK's Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) fined South Staffordshire Water £963,900
(approximately $1.3M) following a 2022 Cl0p ransomware attack that exposed the personal data
of 633,887 customers and employees, later published by the group. Investigators found that
attackers had maintained undetected access to the network for nearly two years before the
breach surfaced.

The fine is notable for its emphasis on prolonged dwell time as an aggravating factor. UK
critical national infrastructure operators face mounting regulatory pressure to demonstrate
active detection capabilities, not just perimeter controls. The Cl0p group is known for
extended reconnaissance periods before data exfiltration and extortion; utilities and water
authorities should treat any endpoint anomaly in OT-adjacent networks as a priority
investigation trigger.
