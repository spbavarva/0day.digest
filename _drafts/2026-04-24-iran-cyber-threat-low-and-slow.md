US officials and security experts have assessed that the most likely Iranian cyber threat to Western
targets is opportunistic, low-profile intrusion activity rather than destructive campaigns. Iranian
actors are conducting quiet intrusions designed to appear larger than they are — a posture favoring
deniability and long-term access over immediate impact.

The assessment shifts the threat model for defenders: rather than preparing exclusively for
destructive wiper attacks or large-scale disruption, organizations should prioritize detection of
persistent, low-noise access and lateral movement. This is consistent with Iran-linked groups' recent
operational patterns (Charming Kitten, Tortoiseshell) that favor credential theft and intelligence
collection over destructive payloads.
