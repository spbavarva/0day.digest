Microsoft says it has observed a surge in attacks using ACR Stealer, an
information-stealing malware family, against its enterprise customers. The
malware is designed to harvest browser-stored passwords, authentication
tokens, and sensitive documents, giving attackers a path to further account
and network compromise via stolen credentials. Organizations should review
endpoint detection coverage for stealer malware and rotate credentials on
any systems suspected of infection.
