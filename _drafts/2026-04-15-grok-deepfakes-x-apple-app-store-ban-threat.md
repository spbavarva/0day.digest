Apple quietly threatened to remove Grok, Elon Musk's AI app, from the App Store in January
over a surge of nonconsensual sexual deepfakes on X. According to NBC News, the threat was made
behind closed doors rather than through public enforcement action. Grok ultimately remained on
the App Store.

The episode illustrates the leverage app store gatekeepers hold over AI products distributed
through mobile platforms, even when platform-level content policies are not formally violated.
It also highlights the gap between AI model safety guardrails and the content moderation
requirements of distribution channels.

For AI safety practitioners, this is a concrete case study in how platform distribution
constraints can serve as an indirect content moderation lever when regulatory frameworks lag
behind AI capabilities.
