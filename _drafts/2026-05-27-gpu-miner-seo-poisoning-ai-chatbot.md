Threat actors are targeting high-performance systems in an active cryptojacking campaign delivered through coordinated SEO poisoning that also manipulates AI chatbot recommendations. The campaign marks a notable evolution in malware distribution: attackers are now polluting LLM outputs alongside search results to surface malicious downloads. Organizations running GPU-heavy workloads — ML training, rendering, data science — are prime targets given the hardware value to cryptojacking operators. Defenders should monitor for unexpected cryptomining processes and verify any software download recommendations from AI assistants against official vendor sources.
