Dutch fitness chain Basic-Fit has disclosed a data breach affecting approximately one million
of its customers. Hackers gained unauthorized access to the company's systems and accessed
member information. Basic-Fit operates across the Netherlands, Belgium, France, Spain, and
Luxembourg.

The specific data categories exposed have not been fully detailed in initial reporting.
Affected members should expect follow-up communication from Basic-Fit and should be alert
to phishing attempts exploiting the breach — fitness and health data combined with contact
details enables targeted social engineering. Credential reuse across other services is also
a risk if account passwords were stored.
