SecurityWeek reports that the Silent Ransom Group is using DNS fast-flux techniques to conceal its command-and-control infrastructure. The group's campaigns have focused on hacking law firms in the United States. Fast flux rapidly rotates DNS records across a large pool of IP addresses, making it significantly harder for defenders and researchers to take down infrastructure or attribute the activity.
