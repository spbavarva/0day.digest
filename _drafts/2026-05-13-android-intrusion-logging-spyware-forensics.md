Google announced Intrusion Logging, an opt-in feature in Android's Advanced Protection Mode
that stores privacy-preserving forensic logs to help detect and investigate sophisticated
spyware attacks. The feature provides persistent logging designed to support digital forensics
teams reconstructing compromise timelines on potentially targeted devices.

Intrusion Logging is aimed at high-risk users — journalists, activists, executives — who face
advanced targeted surveillance tools. Users enrolled in Advanced Protection Mode can enable
the feature now on supported Android versions. Logs can be shared with forensics teams in
the event of suspected compromise.
