Unit 42 assessed the cybersecurity risk landscape for the 2026 FIFA World Cup, identifying ransomware groups, state-aligned threat actors, and opportunistic attackers likely to target critical event infrastructure. The attack surface spans ticketing systems, broadcast networks, transportation infrastructure, and venue operational technology. Organizations providing services to the tournament — payments, hospitality, logistics, and media — should expect elevated and coordinated targeting from groups seeking financial gain, disruption, or geopolitical signaling. The report follows a pattern of major sporting events serving as high-profile targets for demonstrating disruptive capability.
