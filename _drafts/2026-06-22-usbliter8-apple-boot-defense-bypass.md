Researchers released a proof-of-concept exploit dubbed Usbliter8 that bypasses Apple's boot-time defenses and reportedly cannot be patched via software update, affecting millions of iPhones. Because the underlying issue can't be fixed with a patch, mitigation likely depends on future hardware revisions rather than an iOS update. Organizations managing iOS fleets should watch for vendor guidance and monitor for any signs of the PoC being used in the wild.
