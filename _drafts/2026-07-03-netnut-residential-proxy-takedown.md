Google and the FBI disrupted NetNut, a residential proxy service that rented
out access to millions of compromised devices. Cybercriminals and nation-state
actors used the network to route traffic through hijacked devices and mask
their identities during attacks. Residential proxy networks built on
compromised endpoints are increasingly used to evade IP-based detection and
geoblocking in credential stuffing, fraud, and intrusion campaigns.
