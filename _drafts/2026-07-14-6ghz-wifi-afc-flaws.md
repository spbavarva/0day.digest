Researchers describe flaws in Automated Frequency Coordination (AFC) systems
used to manage 6 GHz Wi-Fi spectrum, which by default trust client-submitted
location data. That trust could let an attacker spoof location data to
disrupt spectrum allocation or interfere with critical systems relying on
AFC coordination.

No CVE or vendor patch was cited in the available reporting.
