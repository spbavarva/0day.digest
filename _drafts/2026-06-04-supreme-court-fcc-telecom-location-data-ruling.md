The U.S. Supreme Court upheld FCC fines levied against major telecom carriers for sharing customer location data without consent, ruling 8-1 that the penalties were legal. The Trump administration had backed the FCC's position. The ruling removes regulatory uncertainty for the FCC's authority to act on location data misuse and may influence how similar data-sharing enforcement actions against other carriers are evaluated going forward.
