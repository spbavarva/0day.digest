Volexity has attributed a new espionage campaign to a China-nexus threat cluster it tracks as VerdantBamboo, which it says overlaps with the group Microsoft calls Clay Typhoon. The actor is deploying a BSD variant of the known BRICKSTORM backdoor, alongside two additional malware families codenamed PLENET (aka GRIMBOLT) and AGENTPSD, to compromise Linux-based appliances. Extending to BSD-based systems pushes the group beyond the reach of typical Linux-focused detection signatures, complicating defense for organizations running edge appliances and network devices. Defenders should review Volexity's published indicators for BRICKSTORM and the related malware families.
