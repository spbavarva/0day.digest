The UK's Information Commissioner's Office fined South Staffordshire Water Plc and parent
company South Staffordshire Plc £963,900 (~$1.3M) for a cyberattack that exposed the personal
data of 663,887 customers and employees. The fine is one of the larger ICO enforcement actions
against a UK critical infrastructure operator under UK GDPR.

Water utility operators should review their data protection impact assessments, incident
response documentation, and breach notification timelines. Critical infrastructure organizations
face heightened regulatory scrutiny, and this enforcement action signals the ICO is willing to
levy meaningful fines against utilities that suffer avoidable breaches.
