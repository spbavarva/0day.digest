The ShinyHunters extortion group breached Charter Communications in early April and stole personal information from approximately 4.9 million accounts, per breach notification service Have I Been Pwned. SecurityWeek separately reported that over 42 million records were leaked, suggesting the dataset includes non-account data such as usage records.

Charter Communications, a major US telecommunications provider operating as Spectrum, confirmed the breach. Affected customers should expect credential stuffing and phishing campaigns using the exposed data.

Charter account holders should reset passwords, watch for Spectrum-branded phishing, and monitor credit reports. ShinyHunters continues its pattern of targeting large US telecoms and enterprises — organizations in the sector should audit external attack surfaces for the types of weaknesses that enabled initial access here.
