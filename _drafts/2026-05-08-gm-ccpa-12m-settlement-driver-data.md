General Motors will pay $12 million to settle a California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) complaint related to improper handling of driver data. California officials confirmed it is the largest CCPA fine issued since the law took effect more than five years ago.

The case reinforces that CCPA enforcement is escalating in scale and that automotive telematics data is firmly in regulators' sights. Companies collecting location, behavioral, or biometric data through connected vehicles should treat this settlement as a compliance benchmark for what regulators consider actionable misuse.
