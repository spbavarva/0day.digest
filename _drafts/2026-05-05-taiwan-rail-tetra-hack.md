A 23-year-old university student in Taiwan was arrested for interfering with the TETRA (Terrestrial Trunked Radio) communication system used by Taiwan High Speed Rail (THSR), causing emergency brakes to activate. TETRA is a digital radio standard widely used in critical infrastructure and public safety communications across rail, police, and emergency services. This incident demonstrates that TETRA networks, sometimes assumed to be isolated or obscure enough to be safe, can be targeted by relatively low-resource actors. Rail and transit operators relying on TETRA should review whether their implementations are hardened against unauthorized transmission and consider the implications of this incident for their own threat models.
