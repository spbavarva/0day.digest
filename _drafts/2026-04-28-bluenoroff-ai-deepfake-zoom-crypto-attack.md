BlueNoroff, the North Korean APT focused on cryptocurrency theft, is now deploying a novel social engineering chain: fake Zoom meeting invitations featuring AI-generated avatars and stolen victim video footage. Cryptocurrency executives are the primary target. Once compromised, victims' own likenesses are harvested and reused to lure their contacts — compounding the infection chain.

This marks a significant escalation in AI-assisted social engineering from a nation-state actor. Crypto firms should enforce out-of-band verification for any unexpected meeting link, even from known contacts. Executives should be briefed on deepfake call indicators and suspicious Zoom join flows. Endpoint detection for BlueNoroff malware families should be reviewed against the latest available IOCs.
