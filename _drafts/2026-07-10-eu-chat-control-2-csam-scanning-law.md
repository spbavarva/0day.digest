The European Parliament passed "Chat Control 2.0," legislation permitting
companies such as Google, Meta, and Microsoft to scan users' messages for
CSAM (child sexual abuse material). The law revives a scanning mandate that
had previously stalled amid privacy concerns. Details on scope, opt-in/opt-out
mechanics, and encrypted-messaging carve-outs were not included in the
available summary. Security and privacy teams at affected platforms should
watch for implementation guidance as the law moves toward enforcement.
