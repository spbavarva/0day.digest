LG Electronics USA says it will suspend webOS smart TV apps that turn a
user's television into an always-on residential proxy node, routing
third-party internet traffic through the device. The move follows
research finding that more than 42% of apps and games on LG's webOS store
allow unknown third parties to route traffic through a user's TV.
Residential proxy networks built this way are commonly resold to route
abusive or malicious traffic through unwitting home IP addresses. LG has
not published a removal timeline or a list of affected apps.
