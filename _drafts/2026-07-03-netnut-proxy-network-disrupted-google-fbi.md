A joint operation involving Google disrupted NetNut, a residential proxy
network built on roughly 2 million compromised Android-based devices,
including smart TVs and streaming boxes. NetNut is operated by publicly
traded Alarum Technologies, previously linked to the "Popa" Android
botnet by security researchers. The takedown cuts off proxy access that
let cybercriminals and other actors mask their traffic origin. Owners of
affected smart TVs and streaming boxes should check for unusual network
activity and apply vendor firmware updates where available.
