State-linked hacking groups from China and India separately targeted the
same Pakistani police force between February 2024 and April 2026, in some
cases breaching the exact same systems. The force is responsible for
Balochistan, a province that has been the site of a long-running
separatist insurgency. Overlapping intrusions from unaligned nation-state
actors into the same target systems highlight how contested regions can
attract simultaneous, uncoordinated espionage campaigns rather than a
single dominant threat actor.
