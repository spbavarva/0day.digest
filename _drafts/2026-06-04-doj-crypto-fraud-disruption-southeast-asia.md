The U.S. Department of Justice concluded "Disruption Week" (May 18–June 2026),
a coordinated law enforcement operation targeting Southeast Asia-based
transnational criminal organizations running cyber-enabled cryptocurrency fraud
against Americans. The operation resulted in takedowns of millions of social
media, email, and internet access accounts used by fraud networks, along with
$3.8 million in frozen assets. These operations are commonly run from scam
compounds in Myanmar, Laos, and Cambodia where workers are coerced into
running pig-butchering and romance scams. The action signals continued U.S.
government focus on transnational cybercrime infrastructure, though the scale of
disruption relative to the broader ecosystem remains limited.
