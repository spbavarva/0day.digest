A new analysis describes how attackers are turning the data sources that
autonomous AI agents trust into traps, ranging from hidden content
injections to what the piece calls "cognitive state poisoning." As agents
increasingly act on retrieved or ingested content without human review,
the data they consume becomes as much an attack surface as the code they
run on.
