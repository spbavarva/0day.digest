A U.S. government entity paid about $1 million to a group calling itself
Kairos after a data-theft extortion incident, according to a case study by
Rakesh Krishnan for Ransom-ISAC, built on a leaked negotiation chat and the
blockchain trail the payment left. Researchers found no evidence that Kairos
ever encrypted or "locked" any systems, raising doubts about whether it's a
traditional ransomware gang or purely an extortion actor. The case shows
large payouts can be extracted through data-theft extortion alone, without
an encryption payload.
