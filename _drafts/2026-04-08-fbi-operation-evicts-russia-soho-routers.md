The U.S. government disclosed a court-authorized operation to remediate Forest Blizzard's DNS manipulation campaign across compromised SOHO routers used to breach critical infrastructure. The operation removed Russia's persistent access from affected devices.

The action does not address the root cause — unpatched and end-of-life routing hardware remains a persistent attack surface. This is the latest in a series of FBI-led disruption operations targeting nation-state C2 infrastructure. Organizations with aging edge devices should accelerate hardware refresh cycles and enforce firmware update policies.
