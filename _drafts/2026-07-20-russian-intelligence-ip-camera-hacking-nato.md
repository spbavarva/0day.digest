A Russian intelligence service is systematically hijacking internet-connected
security cameras across Europe and Ukraine to watch military transport
routes, weapons shipments bound for Kyiv, and Ukrainian troop locations. The
finding comes from a joint advisory published July 10 by the AIVD and MIVD,
the Netherlands' civilian and military intelligence agencies. The advisory
frames this as a systematic, ongoing surveillance effort rather than an
isolated incident. Operators of internet-facing camera systems, especially
near logistics routes, should review remote access controls and default
credentials.
