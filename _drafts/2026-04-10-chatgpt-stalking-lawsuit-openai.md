A new lawsuit alleges OpenAI ignored three separate warnings — including the platform's
own internal mass-casualty risk flag — that a ChatGPT user was dangerous before he
went on to stalk and harass his ex-girlfriend. The lawsuit claims the victim also
directly warned OpenAI, but no action was taken to restrict the user's access. The case
directly challenges whether AI platforms bear liability when their own safety systems
generate risk signals that go unacted upon. It is one of an accelerating series of
legal cases linking AI chatbot behavior to real-world harm.
