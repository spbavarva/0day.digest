The UK government will require proof of age—via government ID upload or a facial age scan—before users can create social media accounts, with the ban on under-16s taking effect spring 2027. Security experts warn the checks are easy to circumvent and that verification systems collecting identity documents and biometric data create significant new data-breach attack surfaces. Platform operators bear responsibility for implementing compliant solutions, raising questions about which verification vendors will gain broad access to UK residents' biometric data. The policy is framed as child safety but practitioners flag it as a systemic privacy risk introduced at scale.
