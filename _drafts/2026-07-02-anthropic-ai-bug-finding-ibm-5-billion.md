IBM and Red Hat are assigning 20,000 engineers to a new service called
Project Lightwell, backed by a $5 billion commitment, built around fixing
bugs found by Anthropic's AI. Dark Reading reports the findings come from
Anthropic's Mythos project and have reignited debate over how to secure
the open-source software supply chain at scale. The scale of both the
AI-found bug volume and the engineering commitment signals AI-assisted
vulnerability discovery is moving from research into production-scale
remediation programs.
