Over 900 automatic tank gauge (ATG) systems across the United States — used to monitor fuel and
chemical storage at gas stations and critical infrastructure facilities — are directly internet-exposed
and vulnerable to active attacks. ATG systems with remote access capabilities can potentially be
manipulated to misreport tank levels or trigger false alarms, creating safety risks at fuel depots and
chemical storage sites. Operators should immediately isolate ATG systems from internet-facing networks,
enforce VPN-only access to management interfaces, and conduct inventory of all OT/ICS assets with
external connectivity.
