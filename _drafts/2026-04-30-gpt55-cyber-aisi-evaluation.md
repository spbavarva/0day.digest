The UK's AI Security Institute has evaluated OpenAI's GPT-5.5 for vulnerability discovery and found its offensive cyber capabilities comparable to Anthropic's Claude Mythos. Unlike Mythos—which remains access-restricted—GPT-5.5 is generally available, creating a significant policy gap.

The evaluation assessed the model's ability to find security vulnerabilities, placing it in the same capability tier as Mythos. This means the combination of restrictions Anthropic applied to Mythos provides limited practical protection if equivalent capability is freely accessible via a competing model.

Security teams and regulators should factor capability parity across labs into AI risk assessments. The finding suggests access restrictions on individual models are insufficient without broader coordination across the industry.
