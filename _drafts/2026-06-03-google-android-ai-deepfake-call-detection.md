Google is rolling out an on-device Android security feature that detects AI-synthesized voice in real time during phone calls and flags calls where the caller's voice appears to impersonate someone in the user's contact list. The capability targets social engineering fraud that uses voice cloning to impersonate family members, executives, or colleagues for financial theft. Processing runs locally on-device, avoiding the need to send call audio to external servers. Initial availability is limited to recent Pixel devices, with broader Android rollout expected later in 2026. The feature complements Google's existing scam call detection but focuses specifically on AI-generated voice manipulation.
