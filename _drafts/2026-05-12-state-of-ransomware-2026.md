Kaspersky GReAT's 2026 ransomware state-of-the-union report highlights two major trend shifts:
EDR-killing tooling is now a standard component of ransomware kill chains, and operators are
increasingly forgoing file encryption in favor of pure data-leak extortion — which is faster, leaves
less forensic evidence, and avoids decryption negotiation complexity.

The shift away from encryption toward extortion-only attacks has significant implications for
recovery planning: traditional "restore from backup" response playbooks no longer suffice when the
threat is publication of sensitive data rather than operational disruption. Organizations should
revisit their incident response plans and data classification to understand what an extortion-only
attacker could actually leverage.
