New York has become the first state to enact a moratorium on new large-scale data center construction, with Governor Kathy Hochul citing concerns over electricity costs, water supply, and local control amid the AI-driven building boom. The order blocks new environmental permits for qualifying data centers for up to a year. A separate bill that could restrict development further is still awaiting the governor's signature. The move signals growing state-level pushback against AI infrastructure buildout and could influence other states weighing similar constraints.
