North Korean threat actors stole $280 million from cryptocurrency protocol Drift using
a multi-month operation that began at a cryptocurrency conference, where attackers posed
as employees of a fabricated quantitative trading firm. The fake company was used to
establish trust with Drift staff over roughly six months before the theft was executed.
Post-mortem analysis confirmed North Korean-linked fake companies and financial cutouts
were used to launder proceeds. The operation demonstrates DPRK's continued use of
elaborate cover identities sustained over long periods — a pattern that defeats
standard short-horizon vendor vetting. Crypto teams should apply enhanced due diligence
to counterparties met at conferences, particularly those proposing formal business
relationships.
