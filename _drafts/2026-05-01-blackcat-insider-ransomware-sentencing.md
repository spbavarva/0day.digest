Two former employees of cybersecurity incident response firms Sygnia and DigitalMint have been sentenced to four years in prison each for facilitating BlackCat (ALPHV) ransomware attacks against U.S. companies while employed as defenders with privileged victim access.

The case is the first known prosecution of ransomware "double agents" embedded inside IR firms. Both defendants had deep visibility into victim environments through their incident response roles, which they allegedly exploited to aid the attackers.

Organizations retaining external IR firms should review access controls, ensure least-privilege scoping on third-party credentials, and maintain audit trails of all privileged actions taken during incident response engagements.
