Iranian threat actors have expanded their cyber offensive to include automatic tank gauge (ATG) systems — internet-exposed industrial sensors that manage fuel levels at gas stations, utilities, and critical infrastructure facilities. ATG systems have long been documented as poorly secured, with many accessible directly from the internet using default credentials. Manipulation of ATG readings can trigger false overfill alarms, force emergency shutdowns, or create conditions for physical fuel overflow incidents. This OT/ICS targeting expansion follows a broader pattern of Iranian operations against US and allied critical infrastructure.
