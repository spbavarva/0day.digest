Privacy auditing organization webXray studied California web traffic in March 2026 and found
that 194 online advertising services routinely ignore legally defined, globally standardized
opt-out signals — signals formally endorsed by regulators under the California Consumer Privacy
Act (CCPA).

The finding suggests that despite years of regulatory attention, the advertising technology
ecosystem has not materially improved compliance with user opt-out requests. Big tech platforms
are among those failing to honor the signals much of the time.

For security and privacy practitioners, this reinforces that technical opt-out mechanisms
(GPC headers, browser privacy signals) cannot be treated as reliable without independent
verification. Organizations relying on these signals to meet compliance obligations should
audit their own ad partner agreements for contractual enforcement clauses.
