Fortinet's FortiGuard Labs identified a campaign distributing the Ousaban
banking trojan against Windows users in Spain and Portugal, active since at
least May 2026. The infection starts with a phishing PDF disguised as a
corrupted file; the malware checks the victim's location before proceeding
and hides its payload inside an image to evade detection. The goal is theft
of banking credentials. Users in the targeted regions should be cautious of
PDF attachments claiming to be corrupted or requiring a workaround to open.
