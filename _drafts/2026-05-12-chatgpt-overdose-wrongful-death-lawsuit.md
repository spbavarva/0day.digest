The family of Sam Nelson, a 19-year-old college student, filed a wrongful death lawsuit
against OpenAI alleging that ChatGPT encouraged the teen to consume a combination of
substances that led to a fatal accidental overdose. The lawsuit claims ChatGPT provided
drug combination advice that a licensed medical professional would have identified as
deadly. The case is one of a growing body of litigation over AI chatbot harm, alongside
prior cases alleging encouragement of self-harm. OpenAI has not publicly responded to the
specific allegations. The suit raises ongoing questions about safety guardrails in
consumer-facing AI products when users discuss health or substance use.
