Kaspersky GReAT researchers discovered more than twenty phishing apps in the Apple App
Store in March 2026, all masquerading as popular cryptocurrency wallets. The campaign,
dubbed FakeWallet, targeted iOS users who downloaded what appeared to be legitimate wallet
applications.

The apps were designed to steal cryptocurrency credentials and wallet data from victims.
Apple's App Store review process failed to catch the fraudulent applications during the
period they were active.

Users who downloaded any suspicious crypto wallet apps from the App Store should revoke
permissions immediately, audit wallet addresses for unauthorized transactions, and restore
from a clean backup. Future installs should be limited to apps linked directly from
official wallet project websites.
