A 34-year-old Virginia man was convicted of conspiring to destroy government databases after being
terminated from his federal contractor role. The defendant used residual access — credentials not
revoked at offboarding — to wipe dozens of federal databases in retaliation. The case is a clear
illustration of the insider threat risk created by incomplete contractor offboarding. Organizations
should enforce immediate access revocation upon contractor termination, maintain regular privileged
access reviews, and log all database-level operations for post-incident forensics.
