Google has overhauled its Android and Chrome Vulnerability Rewards Programs, raising the maximum payout to $1.5 million for the most difficult and impactful exploits. Simultaneously, Google is reducing payouts for vulnerability classes where AI-assisted tooling has significantly lowered the barrier to discovery. The dual-track approach reflects a market-based acknowledgment that AI is changing the economics of vulnerability research: rarer, harder-to-find bugs are now worth more, while common-class findings are worth less because they're cheaper to produce. Security researchers focusing on high-complexity Android exploit primitives will find improved incentives in the updated program.
