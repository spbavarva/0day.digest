The UK government has outlined reforms to the Computer Misuse Act 1990 in
briefing documents accompanying the King's Speech at the opening of
Parliament's new session. The proposed changes would create a legal defense for
authorized security research — a protection that does not currently exist in UK
law, leaving researchers exposed to prosecution for activities that are standard
practice in the industry. The reform is bundled within a broader national
security and digital threats legislative package. If enacted, this would align
the UK with the US approach under the CFAA and similar carve-outs in EU law.
UK-based security researchers and red-teamers should monitor the legislative
progress and consult legal counsel on current liability exposure in the interim.
