Law enforcement agencies across the US, UK, and Canada have concluded an international
operation targeting multimillion-dollar cryptocurrency theft schemes, identifying more than
$45 million in associated cryptocurrency and successfully freezing $12 million. No specific
actors, malware families, or exchange platforms have been named in initial reporting.

Cryptocurrency theft operations increasingly rely on phishing, wallet-draining smart contracts,
exchange manipulation, and compromised recovery keys. The operation demonstrates continued
cross-border coordination among Western law enforcement in disrupting crypto-related financial
crime, though the $12M frozen represents roughly a quarter of the identified total.
