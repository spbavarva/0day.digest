UK communications regulator Ofcom has threatened technology executives with jail time
if their platforms fail to adequately suppress AI-generated nudification tools.
The action follows the Grok scandal, in which millions of AI-generated non-consensual
intimate images of women and children were circulated globally. Ofcom cited generative
AI provisions within existing CSAM reporting requirements as the enforcement basis.
This sets a significant precedent: personal criminal liability for platform executives
in the UK for AI-enabled image-based abuse. Similar legislative pressure is building
in the EU and U.S.
