The New York State legislature passed a one-year moratorium on new large-scale data center construction
— the first statewide ban of its kind in the US — pending Governor Kathy Hochul's signature. Lawmakers
cite environmental impact and energy price concerns as the motivation. If signed, the ban would directly
constrain AI infrastructure expansion in New York, where compute demand has been surging. Organizations
with planned data center builds in New York should consult legal counsel on project status under the
moratorium and evaluate alternative sites in neighboring states.
