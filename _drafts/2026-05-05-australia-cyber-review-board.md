Australia has launched the Cyber Incident Review Board (CIRB) to conduct no-fault, post-incident reviews of significant cyberattacks against Australian government and critical industry sectors. The CIRB is modeled on the US Cyber Safety Review Board (CSRB), which was disbanded by the Trump administration in 2025. The board will focus on identifying systemic lessons and improvement opportunities rather than assigning individual or corporate blame. Australia's decision to establish this body as the US equivalent was eliminated is notable, and its findings will be of interest to organizations operating under Australian regulatory frameworks.
