A coordinated law enforcement operation named PowerOFF, involving more than 20 countries and led
by Europol and the US Department of Justice, has taken down multiple DDoS-for-hire platforms and
resulted in four arrests.

These booter and stressor services sell low-cost volumetric denial-of-service attack capability to
hacktivists, extortionists, and nation-state proxies. PowerOFF has conducted several takedown rounds
over multiple years, reflecting both persistent demand for accessible DDoS capabilities and the
difficulty of achieving lasting market disruption when operators can reconstitute elsewhere.
