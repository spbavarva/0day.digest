YouTube has expanded its AI-powered likeness detection feature to Hollywood celebrities,
enabling enrolled public figures and their representatives to search the platform for AI-
generated synthetic media depicting them without authorization and submit removal requests.
The system uses AI to detect and flag deepfake content across the platform.

The expansion signals growing platform-level infrastructure for managing synthetic media at
scale — an engineering response to the rapid drop in cost for generating convincing deepfakes.
The enrollment model (opt-in for public figures) leaves non-enrolled individuals and private
citizens without the same searchable protection.

The broader relevance for security practitioners: deepfake likeness detection at platform scale
is an emerging AI safety capability, and how YouTube validates enrollee identity and processes
removal requests will be a template for similar programs across other platforms.
