Qualys analyzed one billion CISA Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) remediation
records and found that most critical flaws are actively exploited before defenders can
apply patches. The data exposes a structural gap between attacker exploitation timelines
and organizational patch cycle cadences. Human-scale security operations — relying on
monthly patch windows and manual prioritization — are operating outside the remediation
window that matters. The findings reinforce the need for automated, risk-prioritized
vulnerability workflows that treat CISA KEV entries as immediate response triggers
rather than items for the next maintenance cycle.
