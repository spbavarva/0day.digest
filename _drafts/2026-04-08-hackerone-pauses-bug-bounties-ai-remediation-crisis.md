HackerOne has paused bug bounty payouts for open source projects after AI-driven
discovery tooling shifted the bottleneck from finding bugs to fixing them—a cost
that bounties don't fund. Discovery used to be the binding constraint; automated
AI tooling has removed that constraint faster than maintainers can absorb.

This marks a structural inflection point for the vulnerability disclosure
ecosystem. Bug bounty programs may need to evolve toward funding remediation
assistance or scoping more narrowly to complex logic bugs that AI tools are less
capable of discovering independently.
