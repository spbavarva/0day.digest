Federal prosecutors charged a Google security engineer with insider trading after he allegedly used non-public company information to place bets on Polymarket — a cryptocurrency-based prediction market — winning approximately $1.2 million. The scheme exploited access to confidential Google business data that could predict outcomes being traded on the platform.

The case is notable for combining classic insider trading with a novel crypto prediction market venue, and for involving someone with a security clearance level typically trusted with sensitive data. It illustrates that insider threat risk extends beyond data exfiltration to financial exploitation of privileged access.

Organizations should audit what business intelligence their security staff can access and whether monitoring controls flag unusual financial activity correlated with information access patterns.
