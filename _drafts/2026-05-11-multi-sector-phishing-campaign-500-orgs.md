A sustained phishing campaign spanning multiple years successfully targeted over 500 organizations across
aviation, critical infrastructure, energy, logistics, public administration, and technology sectors. The breadth
of cross-sector victimization suggests a sophisticated, likely nation-state-aligned threat actor focused on
long-term intelligence collection rather than financially motivated attackers.

The campaign's multi-year duration without public detection indicates use of low-and-slow techniques that evade
perimeter-focused tooling. Organizations in the listed sectors should review email security controls, check for
legacy credential compromise, and audit trust relationships with sector peers that may have served as lateral
movement pathways.
