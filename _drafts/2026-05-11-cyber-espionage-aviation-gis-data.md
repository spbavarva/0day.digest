A cyber espionage group has been running a sustained campaign against aerospace companies
and commercial drone operators, targeting GIS files, terrain models, and GPS data rather
than conventional intellectual property. The stolen data provides adversaries with precise
terrain awareness and a mapped picture of target environments — information with clear
military and targeting utility.

The campaign has operated without triggering standard detection signatures, suggesting
careful tradecraft or familiarity with victim network baselines. Organizations in
defense-adjacent aerospace, UAV manufacturing, and commercial drone operations should
audit network egress for GIS file format exfiltration and flag unusual volumes of geographic
data leaving internal systems.
