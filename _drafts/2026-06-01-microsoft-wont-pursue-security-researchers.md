Following backlash over its handling of zero-day vulnerability disclosures, Microsoft publicly stated it has "no intention to pursue action against individuals conducting or publishing their security research." The company said it is taking community feedback seriously.

The statement is a notable policy clarification for the security research community, which had grown concerned about legal risk associated with good-faith disclosure. The statement is informal rather than a formal policy document; researchers should still consult Microsoft's Security Researcher Safe Harbor policy for authoritative guidance on scope and protections.
