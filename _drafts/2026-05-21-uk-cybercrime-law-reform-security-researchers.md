The UK government's proposed Computer Misuse Act reform would require security researchers to
cease all activity the moment they identify a potential vulnerability — before confirming it is
real, assessing its severity, or determining exploitability. Experts warn this makes meaningful
vulnerability research practically illegal under UK law.

The proposal would expose researchers following standard responsible-disclosure practices to
prosecution risk. Industry groups are pushing back, but the current draft language has not
incorporated their feedback. UK-based security practitioners should monitor this legislation
closely.
