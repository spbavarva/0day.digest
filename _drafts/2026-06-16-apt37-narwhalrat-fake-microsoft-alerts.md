ScarCruft (APT37), the North Korean state-sponsored threat actor, is running a spear-phishing campaign that impersonates Microsoft Account security notifications to deliver NarwhalRAT, a new remote access trojan providing full system control. The phishing emails simulate account compromise alerts to create urgency and prompt targets to click embedded links. Genians Security Center confirmed the campaign is currently active. APT37 historically targets South Korean individuals, journalists, defector communities, and organizations involved in Korean Peninsula affairs. Targets receiving unexpected Microsoft security alert emails should verify through official Microsoft channels rather than any links in the message.
