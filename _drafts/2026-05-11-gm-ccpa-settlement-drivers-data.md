California Attorney General Rob Bonta announced a proposed $12.75 million settlement with General
Motors over CCPA violations involving the sale of precise driving behavior data — including
hard-brake events, acceleration patterns, and location — to insurance companies without meaningful
consumer consent. The data was collected through GM's connected vehicle programs.

This settlement is notable as a CCPA enforcement action targeting a non-tech company's data
monetization practices in connected products. It signals regulatory appetite to scrutinize
automotive and IoT telemetry under privacy law, with implications for any enterprise collecting
behavioral data from connected hardware.
