Iranian-linked threat actors are actively targeting approximately 4,000 internet-exposed
programmable logic controllers (PLCs) manufactured by Rockwell Automation in attacks
against U.S. critical infrastructure networks. PLCs control physical industrial
processes and their manipulation can cause real-world operational disruption. The U.S.
government has warned that these devices represent a substantial attack surface due to
direct internet exposure. Asset owners running Rockwell Automation PLCs should
immediately verify whether their devices are reachable from the public internet and
apply network segmentation to isolate OT from IT and external networks. The campaign
aligns with Iranian actors' documented targeting of U.S. industrial and utility
infrastructure.
