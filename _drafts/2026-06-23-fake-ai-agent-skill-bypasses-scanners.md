Security firm AIR built a fake AI agent skill and pushed it through a popular
skill marketplace and an Instagram ad to test detection. The skill reportedly
reached roughly 26,000 agents, including some on corporate accounts, while
the payload itself was harmless by design and only collected the user's email
address. Every skill security scanner the firm tested against the fake skill
marked it as safe. The exercise exposes a real gap in current AI agent skill
vetting and marketplace review processes.
