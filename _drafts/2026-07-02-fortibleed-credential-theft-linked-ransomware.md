Researchers have linked the FortiBleed credential-theft campaign, which
exposed nearly 74,000 Fortinet VPN device credentials, to the INC and
Lynx ransomware operations. An operator tied to FortiBleed's
infrastructure was found actively working negotiation panels for both
ransomware groups, connecting the mass credential theft directly to
follow-on ransomware deployment. The finding indicates the stolen,
verified FortiGate credentials were intended to fuel new network
intrusions rather than simply being resold. Organizations with FortiGate
VPNs that haven't rotated credentials since the original leak should
treat this as added urgency to do so.
