Flashpoint's 2026 analysis of tax refund fraud details how criminal actors operate end-to-end
schemes: sourcing stolen PII from breach marketplaces, bypassing government identity verification
systems with synthetic or verified identities, and cashing out fraudulent refund claims through
money mule networks and cryptocurrency channels. The tax season window creates a predictable,
high-volume opportunity that actors prepare for months in advance.

The report highlights how data from prior breaches directly feeds downstream financial fraud —
reinforcing that breach data has long tail consequences beyond immediate credential theft.
Individuals should register for an IRS Identity Protection PIN, monitor IRS online accounts
for unauthorized activity, and treat all unexpected tax correspondence with heightened scrutiny.
Organizations should review insider threat risk around payroll and HR system access during
filing season.
