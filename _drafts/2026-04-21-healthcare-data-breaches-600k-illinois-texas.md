Three US healthcare organizations — Southern Illinois Dermatology, Saint Anthony Hospital, and
North Texas Behavioral Health Authority — have disclosed data breaches collectively affecting
approximately 600,000 individuals. The incidents span providers in Illinois and Texas.

Healthcare remains a high-frequency breach sector due to the value of medical records, PII, and
insurance data on criminal markets. The concurrent disclosure by three separate organizations
in the same reporting window may indicate shared vendor or billing infrastructure exposure,
though this has not been confirmed.

Affected individuals should monitor credit reports, watch for phishing campaigns that may
leverage their healthcare provider affiliation, and enroll in identity monitoring if offered.
Healthcare organizations should review third-party vendor access and assess whether they share
any infrastructure with the affected entities.
