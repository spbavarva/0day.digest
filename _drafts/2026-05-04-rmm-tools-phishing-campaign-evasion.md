Attackers are abusing two legitimate remote monitoring and management (RMM) tools as command-and-control infrastructure in a phishing campaign that has hit over 80 organizations. Because RMM software is commonly allowlisted in enterprise environments, the activity evades most behavioral detection.

Security teams should audit which RMM tools are authorized and actively deployed, restrict RMM software installation to privileged accounts, and alert on RMM processes launched from unusual parent processes or by non-admin users.
