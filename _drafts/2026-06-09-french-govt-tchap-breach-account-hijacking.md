DINUM, France's digital affairs directorate, confirmed that attackers used a hijacked user account to breach Tchap, the French government's internal encrypted messaging platform.
Tchap is used across French ministries for sensitive government communications, making account-level compromise potentially high-impact depending on the victim's access level.
The incident highlights the risk of relying solely on platform encryption when account takeover bypasses it entirely.
Organizations should enforce MFA on all government messaging accounts and monitor for unusual login patterns; session hijacking attacks can undermine E2EE platforms without breaking the encryption.
