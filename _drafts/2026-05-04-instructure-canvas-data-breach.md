Instructure, maker of the Canvas learning management system, disclosed a breach in which hackers disrupted services and exfiltrated names, email addresses, student ID numbers, and messages between users at multiple educational institutions. The company's CISO confirmed the scope on Saturday. Threat actors have threatened to leak the stolen data publicly, suggesting an extortion motivation.

Canvas is among the most widely deployed LMS platforms in higher education and K-12 globally. Affected institutions should notify impacted students and staff under applicable breach notification laws, monitor for follow-on phishing campaigns that exploit the stolen student and staff contact data, and audit Canvas integration permissions for connected third-party tools. Leaked student ID numbers combined with email addresses can be used for targeted fraud and social engineering.
