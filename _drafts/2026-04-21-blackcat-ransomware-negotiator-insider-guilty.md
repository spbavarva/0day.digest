Angelo Martino, 41, of Land O'Lakes, Florida — a former negotiator at cybersecurity incident
response firm DigitalMint — has pleaded guilty to collaborating with the BlackCat (ALPHV)
ransomware gang while ostensibly working on behalf of victim companies. Starting in April 2023,
Martino used his position to help BlackCat operators extract higher ransom payments from five
different victims. He is the third US security professional to admit to aiding a ransomware gang.

The case is a direct supply chain risk in incident response: victim organizations hire external
negotiators to reduce payments and manage the crisis, but those negotiators may have undisclosed
relationships with the threat actors. A double-agent negotiator is positioned to share victim
negotiation posture, financial information, and decision timelines with the attacker.

Organizations engaging ransomware negotiators should verify negotiator credentials and firm
backgrounds, structure engagements to limit information asymmetry, and consider whether the
negotiator has financial incentives that could create conflicts of interest.
