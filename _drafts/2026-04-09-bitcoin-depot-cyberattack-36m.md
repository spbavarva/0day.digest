Bitcoin Depot, one of the largest Bitcoin ATM operators in the US, filed an SEC 8-K disclosing
that a threat actor gained access to internal systems and obtained credentials associated with
the company's digital asset settlement accounts, resulting in approximately $3.6 million stolen.

The attacker leveraged compromised settlement credentials to move funds, pointing to a credential
theft or account takeover vector. Bitcoin Depot has not disclosed the full attack chain or whether
customer funds are at risk. The SEC filing signals the company deemed the incident material under
cybersecurity disclosure rules — a requirement for publicly traded companies since the 2023 SEC
cyber disclosure rule took effect.
