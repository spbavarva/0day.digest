Google announced Gemini Spark, a persistent AI agent designed to work autonomously on tasks for extended periods — up to 24 hours — without ongoing user direction. Hands-on reviewers describe strong agentic task performance but flag concerns about the financial cost and the privacy tradeoffs of an always-on agent with continuous access to personal accounts and data.

Gemini Spark is positioned alongside OpenAI's autonomous agent efforts and Anthropic's agentic Claude capabilities. Security practitioners evaluating AI agents should weigh the expanded authorization scope and data retention surface area that persistent, always-on agents introduce.
