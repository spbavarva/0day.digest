Alec Radford (co-creator of GPT, GPT-2, and Whisper), Nick Levine, and David Duvenaud released talkie-1930, a 13B language model trained on 260 billion tokens of historical pre-1931 English text. Both a base model and an instruction-tuned checkpoint are released under Apache 2.0. The training corpus is entirely out of copyright, giving the base model a clean IP provenance.

The instruction-tuned variant is designed for historical language chat interfaces. The Apache 2.0 license covers both weights and usage, making it one of the more permissively licensed large models for commercial or research applications. The out-of-copyright training data angle may interest legal and compliance teams evaluating LLM deployment risk.
