A new phishing-as-a-service kit called Bluekit, still under active development, incorporates an embedded AI assistant alongside automated domain registration capabilities. The AI assistant is intended to help operators craft more convincing lure content or troubleshoot campaign setup, lowering the expertise barrier for phishing campaigns.

Bundling an LLM into a phishing kit represents an incremental but notable shift in the commercialization of AI-assisted social engineering. The automated domain registration component further reduces friction for operators spinning up short-lived infrastructure. Defenders should watch for increased volume and linguistic quality of phishing lures as such tooling matures.
