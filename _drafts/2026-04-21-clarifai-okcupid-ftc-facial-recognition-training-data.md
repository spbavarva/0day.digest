Facial recognition AI company Clarifai has deleted three million photos it obtained from OkCupid
in 2014 to train its AI models, under the terms of an FTC settlement. Executives from OkCupid
had invested in Clarifai, and the data was shared without the knowledge or consent of OkCupid
users whose photos were used. The FTC settlement marks enforcement action against the practice
of sourcing AI training data through informal corporate relationships that bypass user consent.

The case highlights how AI training data pipelines can quietly draw on user-generated content via
financial relationships between companies — a governance gap that doesn't require a data breach
or technical compromise, just an undisclosed business relationship.

The FTC's willingness to pursue this as a settlements-worthy enforcement action signals broader
regulatory scrutiny of AI training data sourcing practices, particularly where user consent was
absent. Organizations building or procuring AI systems that use personal data for training should
audit data provenance and ensure consent chains are documented and defensible.
