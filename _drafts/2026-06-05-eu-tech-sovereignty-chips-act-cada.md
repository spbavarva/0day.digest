The European Union unveiled a tech sovereignty package comprising Chips Act 2.0, the Cloud and AI
Development Act (CADA), an Open Source Strategy, and an energy digitalization roadmap — all designed
to reduce EU dependence on US and Chinese semiconductor and cloud providers. CADA would impose
significant requirements on cloud and AI providers serving EU customers, including data residency and
supply chain transparency obligations. Organizations with EU data residency requirements should begin
tracking CADA's legislative timeline and assess dual-sourcing or EU-domiciled cloud alternatives as
part of compliance planning.
