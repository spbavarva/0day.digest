Dutch intelligence officials report that at least one Russian intelligence agency is compromising internet-connected cameras across Europe to surveil NATO logistics operations and Ukrainian military personnel. The compromised cameras are reportedly being used to track troop and equipment movements rather than for traditional espionage against IT systems. The advisory underscores how commodity IoT devices with weak security postures are being repurposed for state-level surveillance. Organizations near logistics or military-adjacent sites should audit internet-facing camera systems for default credentials and unnecessary remote access.
