Digital rights nonprofit Access Now and mobile security firm Lookout documented a sophisticated spearphishing campaign targeting two prominent Egyptian journalists, with evidence suggesting the attacker infrastructure is associated with commercial spyware delivery.

The campaign is consistent with state-sponsored surveillance of civil society and press figures. Journalists, activists, and at-risk individuals in politically sensitive environments should use hardened devices, enable lockdown mode on iOS, and audit communications for signs of compromise. The Access Now report contains indicators for detection.
