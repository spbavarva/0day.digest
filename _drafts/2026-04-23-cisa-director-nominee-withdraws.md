Sean Plankey, Trump's pick to lead the Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency, withdrew
from consideration for the director role. Plankey reportedly told colleagues he needed to address
concerns from Sen. Rick Scott (R-FL) about his prior Coast Guard service, which had blocked his
Senate confirmation. No replacement nominee has been announced.

CISA has been operating without a confirmed director for an extended period under acting
leadership. The continued leadership vacancy affects the agency's coordination role in federal
cybersecurity, including its vulnerability disclosure programs, critical infrastructure protection
coordination, and response to active federal network threats.
