India's CERT-In has issued new guidelines requiring organizations to patch critical vulnerabilities in internet-exposed systems within 12 hours where feasible, citing accelerating exploitation timelines driven by AI and LLM-assisted attack automation. The mandate reflects concern that threat actors are using AI tooling to compress the window between disclosure and exploitation.

This is among the most aggressive national patching mandates issued to date. The "where feasible" qualifier provides flexibility but sets a precedent other CERTs may follow. Organizations with significant internet-facing exposure should evaluate whether their vulnerability management pipelines can operationally meet a 12-hour SLA for critical patches.
