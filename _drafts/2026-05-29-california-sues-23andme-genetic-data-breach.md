California Attorney General Rob Bonta filed a lawsuit against Chrome Holding Co. — the entity 23andMe rebranded to after filing for bankruptcy — over its handling of the 2023 breach that exposed sensitive genetic and personal health data.

The lawsuit alleges 23andMe failed to implement reasonable security safeguards to protect highly sensitive information. This is the first major state AG enforcement action specifically targeting the company over the incident, even as it operates under new ownership post-bankruptcy.

The case signals that regulatory pursuit of pre-bankruptcy security failures can survive corporate restructuring. Security teams at companies holding sensitive health or biometric data should note the elevated legal risk even years after a disclosed breach.
