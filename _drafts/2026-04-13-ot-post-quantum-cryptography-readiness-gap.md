Regulatory bodies are requiring operational technology (OT) asset owners to formally attest
to post-quantum cryptographic readiness, but the tooling to accurately assess or achieve that
readiness does not yet exist for most OT environments. The result is compliance paperwork that
overstates actual security posture.

OT systems frequently run proprietary firmware on multi-decade lifecycles where cryptographic
algorithm replacement is far more complex than in IT environments — hardware constraints,
vendor lock-in, and safety certification requirements all inhibit rapid updates. Security
practitioners should treat current OT post-quantum attestations as planning baselines rather
than verified guarantees, and push hardware vendors and system integrators for concrete
quantum-resistant cryptography roadmaps and firmware update paths.
