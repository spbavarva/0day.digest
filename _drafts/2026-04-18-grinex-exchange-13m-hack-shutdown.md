Grinex, a Kyrgyzstan-incorporated cryptocurrency exchange sanctioned by the U.K. and U.S.
in 2025, has suspended all operations after a $13.74 million hack. The exchange attributed
the attack to a "large-scale cyber attack" it claims bore hallmarks of foreign intelligence
agency involvement — a claim that remains unverified.

No technical details about the attack vector have been publicly disclosed. The exchange's
shutdown leaves customers without access to funds and no clear path to recovery.

The intelligence-agency attribution angle is unconfirmed and should be treated skeptically.
The practical impact is a sanctioned exchange ceasing operations following a significant
financial theft, with affected users facing uncertain prospects for fund recovery.
