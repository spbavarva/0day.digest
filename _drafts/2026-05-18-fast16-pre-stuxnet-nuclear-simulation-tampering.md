New analysis by Symantec and Carbon Black has confirmed that the Lua-based `fast16` malware was a
pre-Stuxnet cyber sabotage tool designed to corrupt uranium-compression simulations central to nuclear
weapon design. Its hook engine selectively targeted simulation processes and corrupted results while
appearing to function normally — a hallmark of sophisticated cyber sabotage.

Fast16 is not an active threat, but the findings extend the documented history of state-sponsored
critical-process sabotage predating Stuxnet. The selective process-hooking and silent result
corruption techniques it pioneered remain relevant as a reference for defending high-stakes simulation
and scientific computing environments against tampering.
