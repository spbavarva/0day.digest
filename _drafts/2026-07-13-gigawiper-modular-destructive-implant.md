A newly documented implant dubbed GigaWiper borrows components from
multiple malware families to combine backdoor and destructive wiper
functionality in a single modular tool. The design lets operators choose
between maintaining covert access and executing a destructive attack,
maximizing potential impact while minimizing the operational footprint
needed to deploy either capability. Defenders investigating intrusions
involving this implant should treat either function as a possible
precursor to the other.
