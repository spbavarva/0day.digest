German and US law enforcement dismantled the core infrastructure of
Kratos, described by German investigators as one of the world's most
widely used phishing-as-a-service kits, and Indonesian authorities
arrested the alleged developer. Kratos was built to steal Microsoft 365
session cookies via adversary-in-the-middle phishing pages, letting
buyers hijack authenticated sessions and bypass MFA without a victim's
password or MFA code. Frankfurt's cybercrime unit (ZIT) and Germany's BKA
led the takedown alongside US authorities. The action targeted the kit's
infrastructure and operator, not individual customers who purchased
access.
