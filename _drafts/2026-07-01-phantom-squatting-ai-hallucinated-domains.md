Unit 42 researchers documented "phantom squatting," where attackers
register web domains that large language models hallucinate when asked
about legitimate brands. Because the domains don't exist until an LLM
invents them, attackers can register the made-up address in advance and
host phishing or malware there to catch traffic that AI tools mistakenly
point toward. Unit 42 says the technique is already occurring in the wild
rather than being theoretical, distinct from traditional typosquatting.
