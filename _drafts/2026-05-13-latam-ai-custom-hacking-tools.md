Two threat campaigns targeting entities in Mexico and Brazil have been observed
using AI agents to dynamically generate custom attack tooling during live
intrusions. Unlike static malware, the AI-assisted approach allows adversaries
to adapt payloads and techniques in real time based on the target environment.
This represents a meaningful capability shift: lower-skill actors can now field
bespoke attack chains previously requiring specialist development resources.
The campaigns are characterized as "vibe hacking" — opportunistic,
AI-accelerated exploitation with minimal upfront tooling investment. Defenders
should assume signature-based detection of static malware payloads is becoming
increasingly insufficient as AI lowers the barrier to novel tool generation.
