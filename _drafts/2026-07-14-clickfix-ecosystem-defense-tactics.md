The ClickFix social-engineering technique — which tricks users into pasting malicious commands into a Run dialog or terminal — has grown into a rentable ecosystem available to a wide range of threat actors. The technique evades traditional antivirus and EDR detection, leaving YARA-based analysis as one of the more reliable detection options according to researchers. Its availability for rent at scale means it's no longer limited to sophisticated operators, raising the overall volume of campaigns using it. Security teams should prioritize behavioral detection of clipboard-to-terminal execution over signature-based defenses for this vector.
