Attackers are abusing Apple's legitimate account change notification system to deliver fake
iPhone purchase scam emails. The phishing messages originate from Apple's own servers,
giving them the appearance of authentic Apple communications and allowing them to bypass
many spam and email authentication filters.

The technique does not exploit a code vulnerability in Apple's systems but rather abuses
a notification trigger mechanism to send attacker-controlled content through trusted
infrastructure. This increases the perceived legitimacy of the lure for recipients.

Users should treat any unsolicited Apple purchase notification with skepticism regardless
of sender address. Verify account activity directly at appleid.apple.com rather than
clicking links in email alerts.
