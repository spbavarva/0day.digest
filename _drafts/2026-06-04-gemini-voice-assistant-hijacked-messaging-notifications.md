Researchers demonstrated an attack against Google's Gemini voice assistant where malicious content injected via messaging app notifications could hijack the assistant and trigger dangerous actions—including controlling smart home devices through Google Home and initiating Zoom video calls without user consent. The technique is a variant of prompt injection targeting voice-driven AI assistants through ambient notification channels. Users with Gemini integrated into messaging apps and smart home systems should review assistant permissions and limit which notification sources can influence assistant behavior.
