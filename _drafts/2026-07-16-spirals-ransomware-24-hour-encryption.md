A new ransomware actor calling itself Spirals completed a full corporate
intrusion — from initial access through data theft and encryption — in
under 24 hours. The compressed timeline leaves defenders a much narrower
window to detect and respond before impact, compared to ransomware
operations that have historically taken days to weeks from initial
compromise to encryption. Detection and response playbooks should account
for accelerated dwell-to-impact timelines.
