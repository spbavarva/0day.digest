Data belonging to 500,000 participants in the UK Biobank charity has been listed for sale on a
Chinese website. The exposed dataset includes genetic sequences, blood samples, medical scans, and
lifestyle information — among the most sensitive categories of personal data that exist. UK Biobank
holds one of the world's largest biomedical research databases and the affected participants
originally donated their data for scientific research purposes.

No confirmed attack vector has been publicly disclosed. The severity here is driven by data
category rather than scale: genomic sequences cannot be revoked or changed, making genetic data
breaches uniquely permanent for affected individuals. UK authorities and the charity are
investigating. Practitioners working with health or genomic data should treat this as a reference
case for the sensitivity classification and retention controls applied to such datasets.
