Latvian national Deniss Zolotarjovs was sentenced to 8.5 years in US federal prison for his role as a "cold case" negotiator in the Karakurt ransomware extortion group, which is linked to the Conti and Akira operations. He was extradited to the United States after his arrest and pleaded guilty to money laundering and wire fraud charges. Zolotarjovs was directly involved in extortion strategies and victim negotiations. The prosecution is part of ongoing US efforts to dismantle the Conti ecosystem and its successor groups.
