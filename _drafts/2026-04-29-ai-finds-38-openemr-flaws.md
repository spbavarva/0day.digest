Researchers using AI-assisted analysis discovered 38 security vulnerabilities in OpenEMR, an open-source electronic health records platform used by more than 100,000 healthcare providers worldwide. The flaws include remote code execution, database compromise, and data theft vulnerabilities — a significant attack surface in a platform holding sensitive patient health records.

OpenEMR is a high-value target due to the sensitivity of managed data and its broad deployment across independent clinics and hospitals. Healthcare organizations running OpenEMR should apply available patches immediately and audit exposed endpoints for signs of unauthorized access. The breadth of findings — 38 vulnerabilities from a single AI-assisted audit — suggests the platform has not undergone thorough security review at scale, and further targeted research is likely to follow.
