Estonia will require emails sent from Russia's .ru top-level domain to undergo additional security screening before reaching government officials, according to the country's minister of justice and digital affairs. The quarantine measure is designed to add friction to Russian-origin phishing and influence campaigns targeting Estonia's highly digitized government infrastructure. Estonia, a NATO member, is a frequent target of Russian state-sponsored cyber activity and has historically been among the most proactive EU states in hardening against Russian cyber threats. The screening applies to .ru domain email senders broadly, not only known threat actors.
