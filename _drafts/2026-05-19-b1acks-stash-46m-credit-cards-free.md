The B1ack's Stash dark web carding marketplace released 4.6 million stolen credit card records as a free download, allegedly in response to seller misconduct on the platform.
Free releases of this scale are used to build marketplace reputation and drive traffic, but directly fuel downstream fraud against cardholders.
Financial institutions and card networks should flag potentially exposed BIN ranges for enhanced fraud monitoring and evaluate proactive card reissuance for high-risk segments.
Cardholders whose data was in any B1ack's Stash dataset should monitor accounts for unauthorized transactions.
