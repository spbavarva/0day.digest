The U.S. Treasury Department has announced a new initiative to extend cybersecurity threat
intelligence sharing to the cryptocurrency and digital asset industry. Eligible U.S. digital
asset firms and industry organizations that meet Treasury's criteria will receive, at no cost,
the same actionable intelligence Treasury distributes to traditional financial institutions.

The program treats crypto firms as part of the critical financial infrastructure that warrants
coordinated government threat intelligence support — a notable policy shift from prior years
when crypto businesses operated largely outside established financial sector threat-sharing
frameworks. Digital asset firms should review Treasury's eligibility criteria and enrollment
process to access government-sourced threat indicators relevant to exchange, custody, and
DeFi operations.
