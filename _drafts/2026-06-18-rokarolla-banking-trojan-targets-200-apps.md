A new Android banking trojan dubbed Rokarolla targets roughly 200 financial
and banking applications. Once installed, it lets operators take remote
control of an infected device and harvest sensitive information, including
data tied to banking sessions. The broad list of targeted apps gives the
malware a wide potential victim pool among mobile banking users. No
indication yet of the distribution vector or infection scale.
