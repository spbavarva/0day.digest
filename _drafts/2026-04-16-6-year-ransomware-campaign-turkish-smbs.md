A ransomware campaign targeting Turkish residential users and small-to-medium businesses
has been running continuously for at least six years without significant disruption,
according to new research. The campaign has persisted largely because smaller incidents go
underreported, allowing operators to sustain operations with minimal law enforcement
attention compared to high-profile enterprise attacks.

The longevity of the campaign highlights the asymmetric threat facing SMBs: attackers can
afford low-and-slow operations against smaller targets that fall below the threshold of
major incident response attention. The Turkish focus may reflect regional targeting
preferences or opportunistic exploitation of specific software prevalent in the market.

The research serves as a reminder that ransomware economics extend well beyond the
headline-grabbing enterprise attacks and that under-reporting creates persistent blind spots
in the global threat picture.
