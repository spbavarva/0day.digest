Privacy advocate Max Schrems, founder of noyb, told European officials in
a Tuesday letter that he plans to sue to invalidate the EU-U.S. Data
Privacy Framework following a recent Supreme Court decision. The DPF is
the legal mechanism that currently allows personal data to flow from the
EU to U.S. companies. A successful challenge would be the third such
framework struck down after Safe Harbor and Privacy Shield, forcing
companies that rely on it toward standard contractual clauses or other
transfer mechanisms.
