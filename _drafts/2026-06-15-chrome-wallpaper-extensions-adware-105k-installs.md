Researchers identified 152 Google Chrome extensions, collectively installed more than 105,000 times, that act as "new tab" live wallpaper add-ons while distributing a potentially unwanted program (PUP).
The cluster spans 38 separate Chrome Web Store publisher accounts and three brand backends: tabplugins[.]com, yowgames[.]com, and chromewallpaper[.]com.
The extensions are linked to adware behavior and fake-traffic generation.
Users who have installed "new tab" wallpaper extensions from these publishers should remove them and check for unwanted browser behavior.
