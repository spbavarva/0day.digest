23andMe has agreed to pay $18 million to settle claims brought by a
coalition of 43 state attorneys general alleging the company failed to
adequately protect customers' genetic data. The settlement follows the
company's earlier large-scale credential-stuffing breach that exposed
sensitive genetic and ancestry data, and underscores the legal exposure
companies face for mishandling biometric and genetic data.
