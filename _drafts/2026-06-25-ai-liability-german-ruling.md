A German court ruling held Google liable for errors introduced by its AI
Overviews feature, according to commentary from security researcher Bruce
Schneier highlighted by Simon Willison. The underlying argument: AI agents
act as agents of the organization deploying them and should carry the same
liability as human-authored content. The ruling sets a precedent that
companies can't use "the AI made a mistake" as a liability shield.
