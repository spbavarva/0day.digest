The Canadian Security Intelligence Service used a threat-reduction warrant to access and clean up two foreign-run botnets infecting servers, home routers, and IoT devices on Canadian soil, according to a Federal Court ruling made public on June 15. It's the first confirmed use of CSIS's threat-reduction powers for this kind of direct remote remediation. The warrant authorized CSIS to alter infected devices directly, setting a legal precedent for state intervention in botnet takedowns that other countries' intelligence services may watch closely.
