Cybercriminals have stolen millions from Russian companies by compromising accountants'
workstations and disguising fraudulent bank transfers as salary payments to evade
detection. The largest confirmed single theft exceeded 14 million rubles.

The tactic exploits the trusted access accountants hold over financial systems and
the routine nature of payroll transactions. While the victims are Russian firms,
the technique of targeting finance staff to enable fraudulent wire transfers is not
geographically limited and mirrors patterns seen globally.
