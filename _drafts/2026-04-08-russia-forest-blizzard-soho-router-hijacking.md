A U.S. court-authorized operation disrupted Russia's APT28 (Forest Blizzard)
campaign that hijacked SOHO routers by modifying a single DNS setting to conduct
malwareless cyber espionage against global organizations. The FBI-led action
evicted the threat actors from compromised devices that had been used to breach
critical infrastructure.

The campaign demonstrates that nation-state actors can maintain persistent
network access without deploying traditional malware, making detection
significantly harder. Organizations and ISPs should identify and replace
end-of-life SOHO routers and audit DNS configurations for unauthorized changes.
