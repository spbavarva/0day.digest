A purported leak claiming to expose 5.8 million records of Uruguayan citizens
is the latest in a series of incidents where Latin American cybercriminals have
targeted government agencies to monetize citizen data on underground markets.

The exposed data appears to include personally identifiable information useful
for identity theft and targeted phishing campaigns. Latin American government
databases have become a consistent target driven by monetization through
criminal marketplaces and direct extortion of government agencies.
