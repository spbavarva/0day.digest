Seven families of victims from the Tumbler Ridge school shooting in Canada have filed lawsuits against OpenAI and CEO Sam Altman, alleging the company failed to alert law enforcement after ChatGPT systems flagged activity from the suspected shooter. The suits claim OpenAI was aware of threatening activity and had an obligation to notify authorities but remained silent.

The litigation represents a significant legal test of AI providers' duty-of-care obligations when their systems detect potential harm to third parties. The outcome could set precedent for AI company liability in public safety contexts and influence whether mandatory reporting obligations for AI platforms are legislated. Legal exposure of this type is a new risk category for AI companies that operate at consumer scale with access to user intent signals.
