curl project lead Daniel Stenberg reports that the team is now receiving
security bug reports at 4-5x the rate of 2024 and double the rate of 2025,
averaging more than one credible report per day. The surge is attributed to
AI-assisted security research — reports are notably detailed, long, and of
high quality compared to earlier AI-generated noise.

The volume is unsustainable for a small open-source team: Stenberg notes
his wife has voiced concern about his work hours for the first time in his
career. The curl case is an early concrete signal of how AI tooling is
reshaping the vulnerability research ecosystem — dramatically increasing
pressure on open-source project maintainers who must triage, reproduce, and
fix a much higher volume of valid reports with the same resources.
