Google research finds that indirect prompt injection attempts against AI systems are increasing in frequency, though most attacks remain low sophistication. Some malicious exploits have been identified alongside a larger volume of low-impact probes.

As AI agents become integrated into enterprise workflows, indirect prompt injection — attacks embedded in content the agent processes — is an expanding attack surface. The low sophistication bar means opportunistic attackers are experimenting without deep expertise, a pattern that typically precedes more targeted campaigns. Organizations deploying AI agents should implement input validation, output monitoring, and sandboxed execution where applicable.
