Microsoft's Zero Day Quest 2026 hacking competition concluded with $2.3 million paid out
to researchers who identified more than 80 high-impact vulnerabilities in Microsoft cloud
and AI services. The event carried a $5 million prize pool, with the $2.3M payout reflecting
the volume of valid findings submitted.

The concentration of findings in cloud and AI surfaces is notable — it confirms that
Microsoft's AI-integrated products and cloud infrastructure are yielding a dense attack
surface for skilled researchers. The 80+ figure across a single event suggests systematic
classes of bugs rather than isolated one-offs.

Specific CVEs and technical details from the event will likely surface over the coming
months as Microsoft completes patching and coordinates disclosure with researchers.
