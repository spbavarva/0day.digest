A North Carolina man received a sentence of more than 10 years in federal prison for compiling and selling the personal information of over 7 million elderly Americans to Jamaican fraud rings. The buyers used the data to target victims with phone scams.

The case is one of the largest data broker fraud prosecutions tied to downstream financial harm to individuals. The sentencing reinforces that selling personal data — even without directly committing the fraud — carries significant criminal liability.

Organizations holding large databases of consumer or demographic data should audit third-party data sharing agreements and employee access to bulk export capabilities as insider data theft remains a persistent source of data-for-sale inventory.
