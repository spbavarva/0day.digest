CISA and G7 international partners—Germany, Canada, France, Italy, Japan, the UK, and the
EU—released joint guidance defining minimum elements for Software Bills of Materials
applied to AI systems. The guidance extends the SBOM concept from traditional software to
AI model components, training data provenance, and third-party dependencies embedded in AI
pipelines. The goal is to improve supply chain transparency so organizations can assess
risk in AI systems the same way they assess risk in software packages. Organizations
deploying AI in regulated or critical infrastructure contexts should review the guidance to
align their AI transparency practices before procurement or deployment decisions.
