Two U.S. nationals have been sentenced to prison for operating a "laptop farm" that allowed
North Korean IT workers to fraudulently pose as U.S. residents and gain employment at over
100 companies, including multiple Fortune 500 firms. The scheme funneled salaries back to
North Korea while granting DPRK operatives remote access to corporate environments.

The DPRK IT worker infiltration program is an active, ongoing threat — not a historical
incident. These operations give North Korean state actors persistent insider-level access to
proprietary code, credentials, and internal systems under legitimate employee cover. The
Fortune 500 exposure underscores that the program targets high-value targets, not just
smaller organizations.

Hiring teams and identity verification programs should treat resume fraud and remote-only
onboarding for technical roles as elevated-risk scenarios. The U.S. DOJ has published
indicators and red flags for identifying DPRK IT worker applicants.
