A new analysis of "The Gentlemen" ransomware operation found the group has claimed 478 victims and built worm-like self-propagation into its malware.
The financially motivated group started as an affiliate running double-extortion attacks, drawing on resources and tooling from established ransomware-as-a-service operations including LockBit (Tenacious Mantis), Qilin (Pestilent Mantis), and Medusa (Venomous Mantis).
The worm-like spreading capability raises the risk of rapid lateral movement once an initial foothold is established on a network.
Organizations should prioritize network segmentation and monitor for lateral-movement indicators associated with these RaaS toolsets.
