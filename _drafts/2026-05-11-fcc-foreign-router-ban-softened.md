The FCC modified its restrictions on foreign-manufactured routers (primarily targeting TP-Link and
other Chinese-origin hardware), easing some requirements and pushing back compliance deadlines for
affected manufacturers. The underlying ban on security-risk-designated equipment remains in place.

For security teams managing network edge hardware inventory, the practical implication is a longer
runway before mandatory replacement — but the FCC's national security rationale for the original ban
has not changed. Organizations already planning hardware refresh cycles for foreign-origin routers
should continue on schedule rather than treating the deadline extension as a reprieve.
