Researchers demonstrated a technique called BioShocking that uses context
manipulation to cause agentic AI browsers to abandon their safety
guardrails and exfiltrate sensitive credentials, SecurityWeek reports. The
attack works by manipulating what the AI agent perceives as trusted
context during a browsing session, rather than exploiting a traditional
software flaw. As agentic browsers see wider deployment, this adds to a
growing body of prompt-injection-style research showing they can be
steered into unintended, harmful actions.
