Xu Zewei, 34, a Chinese national alleged to be a member of the Silk Typhoon (Hafnium) state-sponsored hacking group, has been extradited from Italy to the United States to face charges related to cyberattacks against American organizations and government agencies between February 2020 and June 2021, with a focus on COVID-19 research institutions.

The extradition is a rare enforcement outcome against an alleged Chinese state actor. Silk Typhoon was the group behind the mass exploitation of Microsoft Exchange Server zero-days in March 2021 (CVE-2021-26855 and related ProxyLogon chain). The case is significant for demonstrating that Italian mutual legal assistance treaties with the U.S. extend to cyber espionage charges targeting American entities.
