OpenAI is rolling out cloud-based "workspace" agents to ChatGPT Business, Enterprise, Edu, and
Teachers plan subscribers. These agents can perform autonomous business tasks — examples include
pulling web-based product feedback and delivering reports via Slack, or acting as sales automation
agents. The launch represents a step toward AI systems with persistent identities and long-running
task execution inside organizational workflows.

Security teams should consider how agent-to-platform integrations will be governed: OAuth scopes,
API key management, and audit trails for AI-initiated actions are relevant concerns as agentic
capabilities move into enterprise environments. Prompt injection risks in agents with access to
real business systems warrant early attention.
