Foxconn confirmed a cyberattack affecting its North American operations, with factories across
Wisconsin, Ohio, Texas, Virginia, Indiana, and Mexico impacted. The company declined to specify
the number of affected facilities or the nature of the attack, and no threat actor has been
publicly identified.

Foxconn is the world's largest electronics contract manufacturer, assembling hardware for Apple,
Microsoft, Sony, and others. Disruptions to its North American lines could ripple into supply
chains for major consumer electronics brands. Organizations relying on Foxconn components should
monitor for delivery delays and watch for follow-on announcements about data exfiltration.
