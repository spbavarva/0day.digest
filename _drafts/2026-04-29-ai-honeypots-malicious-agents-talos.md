Cisco Talos published research demonstrating how generative AI can rapidly build and deploy adaptive honeypot systems that respond dynamically to malicious AI agents. As threat actors increasingly use AI to automate reconnaissance and exploitation, Talos argues defenders can use AI-generated honeypots to detect, mislead, and study these agents.

The approach enables honeypot environments to mimic real services convincingly, improving effectiveness at capturing novel attack techniques. The research provides a practical blueprint for security teams looking to leverage generative AI on the defensive side — using the same automation advantages available to attackers. This is an actionable AI-in-defense technique worth evaluating for threat intelligence programs.
