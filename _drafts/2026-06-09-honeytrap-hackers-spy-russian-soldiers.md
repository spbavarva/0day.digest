A threat group dubbed SiribClone by Russian cybersecurity firm F6 has been conducting honeypot-style social engineering against members of the Russian armed forces since at least summer 2025.
The operation targets soldiers stationed in border regions and combat zones, with attackers posing as women seeking romantic relationships to gain trust before deploying surveillance-oriented malware.
The technique exploits the reduced operational security posture of personnel under battlefield stress and the relative ease of reaching soldiers via mobile messaging.
The campaign appears designed primarily for intelligence collection rather than disruption, targeting individuals with access to operational information about Russian military positions and movements.
