CISA has added seven vulnerabilities to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog based on
confirmed active exploitation evidence. Notable additions include CVE-2026-21643 (Fortinet
SQL injection), CVE-2023-21529 (Microsoft Exchange Server deserialization of untrusted data),
CVE-2023-36424 (Microsoft Windows out-of-bounds read), CVE-2025-60710 (Microsoft Windows link
following), and CVE-2012-1854 (Microsoft VBA insecure library loading — still being actively
exploited fourteen years after disclosure).

CVE-2026-34621 (Adobe Acrobat/Reader) is also included; patches were released this week.
Federal agencies face mandatory remediation deadlines under BOD 22-01. The presence of the
2012 VBA CVE confirms that legacy Office automation surfaces remain live attack vectors.
Organizations should cross-reference the full KEV list against their asset inventory as a
priority patch-scheduling input.
