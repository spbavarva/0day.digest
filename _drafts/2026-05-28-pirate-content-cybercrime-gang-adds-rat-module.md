Kaspersky GReAT researchers tracking a long-running cybercrime gang that targets
consumers of pirated books and movies report a significant capability upgrade: the
group's previously deployed cryptocurrency miner now includes a full remote access
Trojan (RAT) module. In 2026 the gang expanded to new piracy sites with tens of
millions of monthly visitors. Users who have downloaded pirated content from these
sites may face silent full-device compromise rather than just background mining.
The evolution from miner to RAT marks an escalation in impact and operator intent.
