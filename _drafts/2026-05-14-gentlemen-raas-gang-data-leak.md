An operational security failure within the Gentlemen ransomware-as-a-service group has resulted in
a leak of internal data, offering rare visibility into the gang's organizational structure and
operations. The exposed information reveals a generous affiliate model, opportunistic TTPs, and an
effective hierarchy that helped the group scale its campaigns.

Threat intelligence teams should review the leaked data for affiliate profiles, infrastructure
indicators, and tooling details that may aid in attribution and early detection of future Gentlemen
campaigns. The OPSEC failure mirrors patterns seen in previous RaaS ecosystem leaks (Conti, Black
Basta) and underscores structural vulnerabilities within affiliate-driven ransomware operations
that defenders and law enforcement can exploit.
