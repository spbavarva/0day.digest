Incident responders at Rapid7 attributed an intrusion — initially appearing to be a Chaos ransomware
attack — to MuddyWater, an Iranian APT group tied to Iran's Ministry of Intelligence and Security
(MOIS). The ransomware component appears to have been deployed as a cover to obscure the true
espionage objective and complicate attribution. Using commodity ransomware to mask state-sponsored
activity is a documented tactic by Iranian and North Korean threat actors, as it shifts responder
focus to recovery rather than intelligence collection. Defenders investigating Chaos ransomware
incidents should consider APT involvement and preserve forensic artifacts before remediation.
